# Internal Rollout Guide

Successfully launching new messaging internally before taking it to market. This guide covers who to involve, how to run workshops, what timeline to expect, and how to get everyone aligned and ready to use the new messaging.

## Overview

You've done the hard work of creating positioning and messaging. Now you need to get your entire organization aligned and ready to use it consistently. This is where most messaging initiatives fail. Not because the messaging is bad but because the internal rollout is weak or non-existent.

**Why internal rollout matters:**
- Sales won't use messaging they don't understand or trust
- Marketing will create off-brand content without clear guidance
- Customer Success will confuse customers with inconsistent language
- Executives won't champion what they weren't involved in creating
- Product teams won't align features with positioning if they're in the dark

**What this guide covers:**
- Who needs to be involved (and when)
- The 4-workshop rollout process
- Timeline and resource requirements
- How to handle resistance and build buy-in
- Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

## The Stakeholder Map

Not everyone needs the same level of involvement. Some people need to co-create, others need to be trained, and some just need to be informed.

### Stakeholder Groups

**Core Team (Co-Creators)**
- **Who**: 3-5 people max
- **Role**: Develop the positioning and messaging
- **Involvement**: Every workshop, every decision
- **Typical members**: 
  - Product Marketing (lead)
  - Product Management (product expert)
  - Sales leader (frontline reality check)
  - Sometimes: CEO or CMO (strategic alignment)
- **Time commitment**: 10-15 hours over 2-3 weeks

**Contributors (Reviewers & Refiners)**
- **Who**: 8-12 people
- **Role**: Review, provide feedback, help refine
- **Involvement**: Workshop 2 and 3 (review sessions)
- **Typical members**:
  - Rest of product marketing team
  - Sales engineers
  - Customer Success managers
  - Content/Brand team
  - Additional product managers
- **Time commitment**: 4-6 hours over 2 weeks

**Stakeholders (Need Buy-In)**
- **Who**: 10-20 people
- **Role**: Approve, champion, enforce usage
- **Involvement**: Workshop 4 (final presentation and training)
- **Typical members**:
  - Executive team (CEO, CMO, CRO, CPO)
  - All sales managers
  - CS leadership
  - Marketing leadership
  - Sometimes: Board members or investors
- **Time commitment**: 2-3 hours (one session)

**Broader Organization (Need Training)**
- **Who**: Everyone else
- **Role**: Learn and apply the messaging
- **Involvement**: Training sessions, enablement materials
- **Typical members**:
  - All sales reps
  - All CS reps
  - SDRs/BDRs
  - Marketing team members
  - Support team
- **Time commitment**: 1-2 hours (training + materials)

### Selecting the Right People

**For Core Team:**
- Must have: Deep product knowledge, customer insight, strategic thinking
- Must be: Available for multiple sessions, good collaborators, trusted by the org
- Avoid: Too many people (slows down), HiPPOs who dominate discussions

**For Contributors:**
- Must have: Frontline experience with customers, practical perspective
- Must be: Willing to give candid feedback, able to see different perspectives
- Avoid: Yes-people who won't challenge bad ideas, people too junior to speak up

**For Stakeholders:**
- Must have: Decision-making authority, ability to champion and enforce
- Must be: Willing to commit time to understand and support
- Avoid: Token involvement without real commitment, people who will undermine later

## The 4-Workshop Rollout Process

This process balances speed with thoroughness. It takes 2-4 weeks from start to finish.

### Workshop 1: Positioning Foundation (Core Team Only)

**Goal**: Align on positioning before messaging

**Duration**: 3-4 hours

**Attendees**: Core team (3-5 people)

**Pre-work** (1 week before):
- Core team reads positioning resources
- Each person completes positioning prep worksheet:
  - Who are our best-fit customers?
  - What alternatives do they consider?
  - What makes us different?
  - What value do we deliver that they can't get elsewhere?
  - What market category makes that value obvious?

**Agenda**:

1. **Intro & Context** (30 min)
   - Why we're doing this work
   - The positioning framework (April Dunford's 5 components)
   - How this feeds into messaging
   - Ground rules for the workshop

2. **Best-Fit Customers** (45 min)
   - Who gets the most value from our product?
   - What characteristics do they share?
   - Who should we NOT be selling to?
   - Converge on 1-2 primary customer profiles

3. **Competitive Alternatives** (45 min)
   - What do people use today (including doing nothing)?
   - What would they buy if we didn't exist?
   - Map the competitive landscape
   - Identify our most common head-to-head competitors

4. **Differentiated Capabilities** (45 min)
   - What can we do that alternatives can't?
   - What do we do better than anyone else?
   - What's actually defensible vs nice-to-have?
   - Rank by importance to customers

5. **Differentiated Value** (45 min)
   - What value do those capabilities deliver?
   - Why does that matter to our best-fit customers?
   - How do we quantify that value?
   - Connect capabilities to business outcomes

6. **Market Category** (30 min)
   - What category makes our value obvious?
   - Are we creating a new category or joining an existing one?
   - How do customers describe what we do?
   - Test: Does the category help or hurt positioning?

**Output**: Draft positioning statement and rationale for key decisions

**Follow-up**: 
- Product Marketing documents decisions
- Circulates to core team for async refinement
- Prepares for Workshop 2

### Workshop 2: Messaging Development (Core Team + Contributors)

**Goal**: Create messaging pillars, proof points, and persona variations

**Duration**: 3 hours

**Attendees**: Core team (3-5) + Contributors (8-12)

**Pre-work** (3-5 days before):
- Share Workshop 1 positioning output
- Contributors review and submit questions/concerns
- Core team prepares initial messaging draft

**Agenda**:

1. **Positioning Recap** (20 min)
   - Walk through positioning decisions from Workshop 1
   - Address questions
   - Get alignment before moving to messaging

2. **Messaging Pillars Brainstorm** (40 min)
   - What are the 3-5 core themes that support our positioning?
   - Generate options (diverge)
   - Group similar ideas
   - Discuss and debate
   - Vote on top pillars

3. **Proof Points** (60 min)
   - Break into small groups (one per pillar)
   - For each pillar: What's the evidence?
   - Features, data, customer stories, competitive advantages
   - Report back to full group
   - Refine and strengthen

4. **Persona Variations** (45 min)
   - Review P1 (User), P2 (Champion), P3 (Economic Buyer) framework
   - For each pillar: How does it land differently by persona?
   - What does P1 care about vs P3?
   - How do we adapt emphasis, language, proof points?

5. **Testing Plan** (15 min)
   - How will we test this messaging?
   - Who will we test with?
   - What's the timeline?

**Output**: Messaging pillars with proof points and persona variations

**Follow-up**:
- Product Marketing writes up full messaging framework
- Conducts message testing (see core file 07)
- Prepares refined version for Workshop 3

### Workshop 3: Refinement & Application (Contributors + Stakeholders)

**Goal**: Refine messaging based on feedback and test message application

**Duration**: 2 hours

**Attendees**: Contributors (8-12) + Stakeholders (10-20)

**Pre-work** (1 week before):
- Share full messaging framework document
- Include test results if available
- Ask for written feedback in advance

**Agenda**:

1. **Messaging Overview** (20 min)
   - Walk through positioning → pillars → proof points
   - Show how it addresses different personas
   - Share any test results

2. **Feedback & Refinement** (40 min)
   - What's working? What's not?
   - Where does it feel off?
   - What's confusing or unclear?
   - What's missing?
   - Capture all feedback (don't defend, just listen)

3. **Application Exercise** (45 min)
   - Break into small groups by function
   - Sales team: How would you use this in a first call?
   - CS team: How does this change customer conversations?
   - Marketing: What content does this inspire?
   - Product: How does this influence roadmap prioritization?
   - Report back challenges and opportunities

4. **Objection Handling** (15 min)
   - What objections will we hear?
   - How does our messaging address them?
   - What's missing from objection handling perspective?

**Output**: Refined messaging + understanding of application challenges

**Follow-up**:
- Product Marketing makes final refinements
- Creates enablement materials
- Prepares for final rollout workshop

### Workshop 4: Rollout & Training (All Stakeholders + Broader Org)

**Goal**: Official launch, train everyone, get commitment to use

**Duration**: 2 hours

**Attendees**: Everyone who needs to use the messaging

**Pre-work** (3-5 days before):
- Share final messaging framework
- Send "what's changing" overview
- Set expectations for the workshop

**Agenda**:

1. **Why We're Here** (10 min)
   - The business case for new messaging
   - What wasn't working before
   - What success looks like

2. **The New Messaging** (30 min)
   - Walk through complete framework
   - Positioning statement
   - Messaging pillars with proof points
   - How it adapts by persona
   - Examples of good usage

3. **Channel-Specific Application** (30 min)
   - Break out by function:
     - **Sales**: How to use in discovery, demo, close
     - **Marketing**: How to adapt for web, email, ads, social
     - **CS**: How to use in onboarding, support, renewal
     - **Product**: How this influences feature naming, in-product copy
   - Each group creates 2-3 examples
   - Share back with full group

4. **Resources & Tools** (20 min)
   - Walk through enablement resources:
     - Messaging house (one-pager)
     - Sales deck template
     - Email templates
     - Battle cards
     - FAQs
   - Where to find everything
   - Who to ask for help

5. **Rollout Plan** (15 min)
   - What's changing when
   - Phased approach (internal first, then external)
   - Timeline for updating materials
   - How we'll measure success

6. **Q&A & Commitment** (15 min)
   - Address concerns
   - Get explicit commitment from leaders to champion and enforce
   - Celebrate the work

**Output**: Trained organization ready to use new messaging

**Follow-up**:
- Share recording and materials
- Set up office hours for questions
- Begin phased external rollout (see core file 08)

## Timeline & Resource Requirements

### Typical Timeline (Total: 2-4 weeks)

**Week 1**:
- Workshop 1 (positioning)
- Core team refinement

**Week 2**:
- Workshop 2 (messaging development)
- Message testing begins (parallel track)
- Contributors provide async feedback

**Week 3**:
- Testing continues
- Messaging refinement based on feedback
- Enablement materials creation begins
- Workshop 3 (refinement & application)

**Week 4**:
- Final materials finalized
- Workshop 4 (rollout & training)
- Begin internal usage

### Fast Track (2 weeks)

Compress Weeks 2 and 3:
- Run Workshop 2 and 3 in same week
- Skip formal message testing (risky but doable)
- Parallel enablement creation

**Trade-offs**:
- Less refinement time
- No external validation
- Higher risk of missing something
- May need to fix messaging post-launch

### Thorough Track (4+ weeks)

Add time between workshops:
- More thorough message testing (2 weeks)
- Multiple rounds of refinement
- Create more comprehensive enablement materials
- Run additional feedback cycles

**Benefits**:
- Higher quality messaging
- Better buy-in
- More comprehensive enablement
- Lower risk of needing major changes

### Resource Requirements

**Product Marketing (Lead)**:
- Full-time for 2-4 weeks
- Owns the process, facilitates workshops, creates materials

**Core Team**:
- 10-15 hours total spread over 2-3 weeks
- Active participation, async feedback

**Contributors**:
- 4-6 hours total spread over 1-2 weeks
- Workshop attendance, async feedback

**Stakeholders**:
- 2-3 hours total
- Workshop 4 attendance, review of materials

**External Support** (Optional):
- Messaging consultant: Facilitate workshops ($10-20K)
- Message testing platform: Wynter, UserTesting ($2-5K)
- Designer: Create enablement materials ($3-5K)

## Handling Resistance & Building Buy-In

Every rollout faces resistance. The key is understanding why people resist and addressing it proactively.

### Common Sources of Resistance

**1. "This isn't how we do things"**
- **Who**: Long-tenured employees, especially those who created current messaging
- **Why**: Emotional investment in status quo, fear of change
- **How to address**:
  - Acknowledge what worked about old messaging
  - Show data on what's not working anymore
  - Frame as evolution, not rejection of the past
  - Involve them in creating the new (Workshop 2)

**2. "Our customers are different"**
- **Who**: Sales reps who think their territory is unique
- **Why**: Experience doesn't match the new positioning
- **How to address**:
  - Use data to show the patterns
  - Create persona variations that address edge cases
  - Let them test messaging and provide feedback
  - Build in flexibility for customization

**3. "This won't work in my situation"**
- **Who**: Individual contributors who see practical barriers
- **Why**: Legitimate concerns about application
- **How to address**:
  - Run application exercises (Workshop 3)
  - Create role-specific examples and templates
  - Address objections explicitly
  - Provide ongoing support and office hours

**4. "We don't have time for this"**
- **Who**: Busy executives, quota-carrying reps
- **Why**: Don't see the ROI on time investment
- **How to address**:
  - Show cost of current inconsistency
  - Demonstrate time savings from clear messaging
  - Keep workshops efficient and productive
  - Provide async alternatives when possible

**5. "This is too risky"**
- **Who**: Risk-averse executives, product leaders
- **Why**: Fear of confusing customers, losing deals
- **How to address**:
  - Use message testing data
  - Propose phased rollout (test before full launch)
  - Show examples from comparable companies
  - Build in feedback loops and adjustment plans

**6. "I wasn't involved, so I don't trust it"**
- **Who**: Key stakeholders not in core or contributor groups
- **Why**: Feeling excluded from important decisions
- **How to address**:
  - Involve them in Workshop 3 or 4
  - Seek their input early (even before Workshop 1)
  - Acknowledge their expertise
  - Give them ownership of implementation in their area

### Strategies to Build Buy-In

**Before the Process Starts**:

1. **Get Executive Sponsorship**
   - Identify one executive champion (ideally CMO or CRO)
   - Get explicit commitment to support and enforce
   - Have them communicate why this matters
   - Secure their participation in key workshops

2. **Socialize the Need**
   - Share data on current messaging problems:
     - Sales feedback on unclear value prop
     - Long sales cycles
     - Customer confusion
     - Inconsistent messaging across channels
   - Create a sense of urgency without creating panic
   - Frame as opportunity, not crisis

3. **Set Clear Expectations**
   - Be explicit about time commitment
   - Explain the process and timeline
   - Clarify roles and decision-making authority
   - Address "what's in it for me" for each group

**During the Process**:

1. **Make it Collaborative, Not Top-Down**
   - Ask questions, don't dictate answers
   - Genuinely consider all input
   - Explain decisions, even when you overrule
   - Give credit publicly for good ideas

2. **Show Your Work**
   - Don't present messaging as fait accompli
   - Walk through the logic and tradeoffs
   - Share customer research and competitive analysis
   - Be transparent about what you're uncertain about

3. **Use Data, Not Opinions**
   - Ground debates in facts:
     - Customer quotes
     - Win/loss analysis
     - Message test results
     - Competitive research
   - When data is absent, run tests before deciding
   - Acknowledge when you're making judgment calls

4. **Address Concerns Directly**
   - Don't dismiss objections
   - Dig into the "why" behind resistance
   - Either adjust messaging or explain why you're not
   - Circle back to show you heard and responded

**After the Process**:

1. **Follow Through**
   - Actually use the new messaging
   - Update all materials as promised
   - Provide ongoing support
   - Celebrate early wins

2. **Enforce Consistency**
   - Have executives model usage
   - Call out off-brand messaging (gently at first)
   - Regularly review external communications
   - Make it easy to do the right thing

3. **Create Feedback Loops**
   - Regular check-ins with sales and CS
   - Monitor message performance
   - Adjust based on real-world learning
   - Show that the messaging evolves

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

### Pitfall 1: Skipping Internal Rollout Entirely

**What it looks like**:
- Product Marketing creates messaging alone
- Sends it out via email or Slack
- Expects everyone to use it
- Wonders why adoption is low

**Why it fails**:
- No buy-in or understanding
- People don't trust what they didn't help create
- Messaging doesn't reflect frontline reality
- No training on how to use it

**How to avoid**: 
- Always run at least Workshop 1, 2, and 4
- Can compress timeline, but can't skip involvement

### Pitfall 2: Too Many Cooks

**What it looks like**:
- Core team of 10+ people
- Every stakeholder demands input
- Design-by-committee
- Weak, consensus messaging that pleases no one

**Why it fails**:
- Decision-making is impossible
- Lowest common denominator thinking
- No clear ownership
- Endless rounds of revision

**How to avoid**:
- Keep core team to 3-5 max
- Expand involvement in Workshop 2 and 3
- Clear decision rights (Product Marketing leads)
- Set deadline for when debate ends

### Pitfall 3: Death by Workshops

**What it looks like**:
- 6-8 workshops over 3 months
- Same discussions repeatedly
- Analysis paralysis
- Momentum dies, people check out

**Why it fails**:
- People have actual jobs to do
- Diminishing returns on additional input
- Window for change closes
- Organizational patience runs out

**How to avoid**:
- Stick to 4-workshop process
- Make decisions and move on
- Use async feedback between workshops
- Set and hold to timeline

### Pitfall 4: No Executive Air Cover

**What it looks like**:
- Product Marketing leads rollout alone
- No executive sponsorship or enforcement
- Leaders don't use new messaging
- Teams revert to old habits

**Why it fails**:
- Without top-down support, adoption is optional
- People follow what leaders model, not what they say
- Old messaging creeps back in
- Inconsistency returns

**How to avoid**:
- Secure executive sponsor before starting
- Have executives actively participate in workshops
- Get public commitment to use and enforce
- Ask leaders to model the change

### Pitfall 5: Training Without Materials

**What it looks like**:
- Great workshop, everyone is excited
- But no templates, examples, or resources
- People don't know how to apply it
- Adoption stalls

**Why it fails**:
- People are busy and default to what's easy
- Without templates, they recreate from memory
- Inconsistency creeps in immediately
- Good intentions don't translate to usage

**How to avoid**:
- Create enablement materials BEFORE Workshop 4
- Provide role-specific examples
- Make it easier to use new messaging than old
- Give people templates to copy/paste

### Pitfall 6: Launch and Abandon

**What it looks like**:
- Big rollout workshop
- Then radio silence
- No follow-up or reinforcement
- New messaging fades away in weeks

**Why it fails**:
- Behavior change requires reinforcement
- Questions arise during application
- Old habits are strong
- Without ongoing support, people revert

**How to avoid**:
- Office hours for first 2 weeks post-launch
- Regular reminders in team meetings
- Celebrate good examples publicly
- Audit materials quarterly for consistency

### Pitfall 7: Messaging Doesn't Match Reality

**What it looks like**:
- Beautiful messaging framework
- But product doesn't deliver on promises
- Sales can't close deals with it
- Customers don't relate

**Why it fails**:
- Aspirational positioning without foundation
- Product and messaging misaligned
- Credibility destroyed by overpromising
- Market doesn't see what you claim

**How to avoid**:
- Test messaging with real customers (core file 07)
- Include product team in core group
- Be honest about current vs future state
- Only claim what you can prove

## Measuring Success

How do you know if your internal rollout worked?

### Leading Indicators (First 30 days)

**Adoption Metrics**:
- % of sales decks using new messaging
- % of email templates updated
- % of website pages updated
- % of sales reps using new pitch in calls

**Understanding Metrics**:
- Post-training quiz scores
- Questions asked in office hours
- Feedback survey results
- Spot-check conversations (listen to calls)

**Engagement Metrics**:
- Training attendance rate
- Materials download rate
- Active users in messaging repository
- Slack channel activity

### Lagging Indicators (60-90 days)

**Consistency Metrics**:
- Audit of external materials (% on-brand)
- Mystery shop calls (reps using pillars)
- Customer conversations (CS using language)
- Content review (blog posts, social, ads)

**Effectiveness Metrics**:
- Sales cycle length (shorter?)
- Win rates (higher?)
- Deal velocity (faster?)
- Customer understanding ("we get it" feedback)

**Sales Confidence Metrics**:
- Rep confidence scores (survey)
- Objection handling effectiveness
- Feature attachment rates
- Competitive win rates

### Red Flags

Signs your rollout isn't working:

- Sales reps creating their own messaging
- Executives using off-brand language publicly
- Customers confused about what you do
- No one referencing the messaging framework
- Materials reverting to old positioning
- Rep feedback: "This doesn't work with my accounts"

If you see these, don't wait. Diagnose and fix quickly:
- Gather feedback (why isn't it working?)
- Adjust messaging or change rollout approach
- Provide additional training
- Get executive re-commitment
