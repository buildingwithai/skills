# Positioning Canvas Template

**Purpose:** Capture your product positioning using April Dunford's 5-component framework

**When to use:** After completing positioning workshop or when documenting positioning

**Time to complete:** 30-60 minutes (after workshop/research)

## Instructions

1. Complete each section below
2. Use customer language, not internal jargon
3. Be specific - avoid vague generalities
4. Test with customers to validate
5. Iterate based on feedback

## Positioning Statement

**One-sentence positioning statement:**

"For [best-fit customers], [product name] is a [market category] that [differentiated value]. Unlike [competitive alternative], [product name] [unique capability]."

**Example:**
"For fast-growing B2B SaaS sales teams, SalesForce is a CRM that helps reps close more deals with better visibility. Unlike spreadsheets, SalesForce provides automated tracking and pipeline management."

## Validation Checklist

Before finalizing, validate your positioning:

- [ ] **Best-fit customers** are specific (not "everyone")
- [ ] **Alternatives** are what customers actually consider (not just competitors)
- [ ] **Unique capabilities** are provable and defensible
- [ ] **Value** ties directly to unique capabilities
- [ ] **Value** is expressed in customer outcomes (not features)
- [ ] **Category** makes value immediately obvious
- [ ] **Positioning statement** is clear and differentiated
- [ ] Tested with at least 5 customers in target segment
- [ ] Sales team can explain it consistently

---

## Next Steps

After completing positioning:

1. **Validate with customers** - Test with 5-10 best-fit customers
2. **Get internal alignment** - Share with exec team, sales, product
3. **Move to messaging** - Use `messaging` skill to translate positioning into messages
4. **Document** - Save this canvas and reference in all marketing/sales materials

---

## Tips

**Be specific:**
- ❌ Bad: "Enterprise customers"
- ✅ Good: "Series B-C SaaS companies with 200-500 employees"

**Use customer language:**
- ❌ Bad: "AI-powered predictive analytics engine"
- ✅ Good: "Tells you which deals will close this month"

**Focus on outcomes:**
- ❌ Bad: "Advanced reporting features"
- ✅ Good: "Sales managers get visibility without manual spreadsheets"

**Prove your uniqueness:**
- ❌ Bad: "Better user experience"
- ✅ Good: "Only platform with one-click integrations to all major tools"
