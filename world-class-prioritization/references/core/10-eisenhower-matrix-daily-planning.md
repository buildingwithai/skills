# Eisenhower Matrix & Daily Planning

*"What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important." — Dwight D. Eisenhower*

## The Problem: Everything Feels Urgent

**Your day:**
- Email pinging constantly
- Slack messages demanding attention
- Meetings back-to-back
- "Quick question" interruptions
- Actual important work? When?

**You're busy all day. You're exhausted. But what did you actually accomplish?**

**The Eisenhower insight:** Most people confuse urgent with important.

## The Four Quadrants

```
            │ Urgent │ Not Urgent
────────────┼────────┼──────────────
Important   │   Q1   │      Q2
            │ Crisis │   Strategic
            │   DO   │  SCHEDULE
────────────┼────────┼──────────────
Not         │   Q3   │      Q4
Important   │  Noise │    Waste
            │DELEGATE│  ELIMINATE
```

### Q1: Urgent & Important (Crisis)
**Examples:**
- Production system down
- Customer escalation (major account)
- Deadline tomorrow
- Medical emergency
- Legal/compliance issue

**How to handle:** Do immediately. Drop everything else.

**Goal:** Minimize time in Q1 (crises are unavoidable, but many are preventable).

### Q2: Not Urgent & Important (Strategic)
**Examples:**
- Planning, strategy work
- Relationship building (team, customers, stakeholders)
- Learning, skill development
- Exercise, health
- Deep work on major projects
- Prevention (avoiding future Q1 crises)
- Important conversations you've been avoiding

**How to handle:** Schedule it. Protect the time. This is where effectiveness lives.

**Goal:** Maximize time in Q2 (50%+ of your time should be here).

### Q3: Urgent & Not Important (Distraction)
**Examples:**
- Most emails
- Most meetings
- Some phone calls
- Status update requests
- Other people's priorities
- Interruptions

**How to handle:** Delegate, minimize, batch.

**Goal:** Eliminate or delegate most Q3 activities.

**The trap:** Q3 FEELS like Q1 (it's urgent!), but it's not moving your needle. This is where most people waste their time.

### Q4: Not Urgent & Not Important (Waste)
**Examples:**
- Mindless social media scrolling
- Busywork (activity ≠ productivity)
- Excessive TV/Netflix
- Pointless meetings
- Trivia, gossip

**How to handle:** Eliminate completely.

**Goal:** Zero time in Q4.

## How to Categorize Tasks Correctly

**The key questions:**

### Is it URGENT?
**"What happens if I don't do this today/this week?"**
- Nothing bad → Not urgent
- Minor inconvenience → Not urgent
- Major problem → Urgent

**Red flag:** Just because someone WANTS it urgently doesn't make it urgent. Ask: Is this truly time-sensitive?

### Is it IMPORTANT?
**"Does this move me toward my goals?"**
- Yes, directly → Important
- No, but someone asked → Probably not important (to YOU)
- Unclear → Check alignment with values/goals

**Red flag:** Busy ≠ important. You can be very busy with Q3/Q4 tasks and achieve nothing.

## Common Categorization Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Treating all urgent things as important
**Reality:** Q3 is urgent but NOT important.

**Example:**
- "Quick question" Slack message → Urgent (to them), not important (to you)
- Most meetings → Urgent (scheduled time), not important (no decisions made)

**Fix:** Ask "Is this urgent AND important, or just urgent?"

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Q2 because nothing is on fire
**Reality:** Q2 prevents Q1 fires.

**Example:**
- Exercise → Not urgent, but important (prevents health crisis)
- Planning → Not urgent, but prevents future chaos
- Relationship building → Not urgent, but enables future collaboration

**Fix:** Schedule Q2 time. Protect it fiercely.

### Mistake 3: Thinking Q3 is your job
**Reality:** Q3 is often other people's priorities, not yours.

**Example:**
- Status update emails → Important to your manager, not to your actual work
- Some meetings → Important to organizer, not to you

**Fix:** Delegate Q3 or say no.

## The Q2 Focus Strategy

**The secret to effectiveness: Live in Q2.**

**Why Q2 matters:**
- Q2 work prevents Q1 crises
- Q2 builds long-term success
- Q2 is proactive (not reactive)
- Q2 aligns with your values/goals

**How to create Q2 time:**

**1. Schedule it**
Block calendar time for Q2 activities. Treat it like a meeting.
> Monday 9-11am: Deep work on Q2 project
> Wednesday 2-3pm: Strategic planning
> Friday 8-9am: Learning/reading

**2. Protect it**
Say no to Q3/Q4 activities that encroach.
> "I have a prior commitment" (your Q2 time is a commitment to yourself)

**3. Start with 2 hours/day**
You don't need to go from 0 to 8 hours immediately.
> Start: 2 hours of Q2 per day
> Build up: 4-5 hours of Q2 per day (the rest is Q1/Q3)

**4. Track it**
At end of day: "How much time did I spend in Q2 today?"
- <1 hour → Problem
- 2-3 hours → Good
- 4+ hours → Excellent

## Daily Planning with Eisenhower Matrix

**Every morning (or night before), categorize your tasks:**

**Step 1: Brain dump all tasks**
Write down everything on your plate today.

**Step 2: Categorize into quadrants**
For each task: Urgent? Important? → Place in correct quadrant.

**Step 3: Plan your day**
- **Q1:** Handle these first (crises)
- **Q2:** Schedule these (2+ hours minimum)
- **Q3:** Delegate, batch, or decline
- **Q4:** Delete

**Step 4: Protect Q2 time**
Don't let Q3 interrupt Q2 blocks.

**Example daily plan:**

**Morning:**
- 8-9am: Handle Q1 (customer escalation) ✅ Crisis
- 9-12pm: Q2 deep work (product strategy doc) ✅ Strategic

**Afternoon:**
- 12-1pm: Lunch + walk (Q2 - health)
- 1-2pm: Batch Q3 (emails, Slack, status updates) ✅ Batched noise
- 2-5pm: Q2 work (finish strategy, prep for launch) ✅ Strategic
- 5pm: Done. Q4 eliminated.

**Notice:** Q2 got 5+ hours. Q3 was batched into 1 hour. Q1 was handled quickly. Q4 was eliminated.

## Weekly Eisenhower Review

**Every week, audit where your time actually went:**

**Step 1: Track time for one week**
At end of each day, estimate:
- Q1: X hours
- Q2: X hours
- Q3: X hours
- Q4: X hours

**Step 2: Calculate percentages**
Example:
- Q1: 15% (6 hours)
- Q2: 35% (14 hours)
- Q3: 40% (16 hours) ← Problem!
- Q4: 10% (4 hours)

**Step 3: Identify problems**
- **Too much Q1?** → Need more Q2 prevention work
- **Too much Q3?** → Need better boundaries, delegation, saying no
- **Any Q4?** → Eliminate immediately
- **Not enough Q2?** → Schedule it explicitly

**Step 4: Adjust next week**
- Reduce Q3: Decline 3 meetings, delegate 2 tasks
- Increase Q2: Block 2-hour morning slots Mon/Wed/Fri
- Eliminate Q4: Delete social media apps, no TV on weeknights

## The Q1/Q2 Reduction Flywheel

**More Q2 → Less Q1**

**How Q2 prevents Q1:**
- **Planning** (Q2) prevents **crises** (Q1)
- **Relationship building** (Q2) prevents **conflicts** (Q1)
- **Learning** (Q2) prevents **skill gaps** (Q1)
- **Exercise** (Q2) prevents **illness** (Q1)
- **Maintenance** (Q2) prevents **breakage** (Q1)

**The virtuous cycle:**
- Spend more time in Q2
- Fewer Q1 crises occur
- More time available for Q2
- Even fewer Q1 crises
- Upward spiral of effectiveness

## Common Objections

**"But my job IS Q1/Q3!"**
→ Is it really? Or have you trained people to expect immediate responses? Audit your time honestly. Most people overestimate Q1, underestimate Q3.

**"I can't ignore urgent requests!"**
→ Some are truly urgent (Q1). Most are not (Q3). Learn to distinguish. See `07-saying-no-professionally.md`.

**"Q2 feels less productive because nothing is on fire"**
→ Q2 IS the most productive. It's just less visible. Q1/Q3 create the illusion of productivity (busy ≠ effective).

**"I don't have time for Q2!"**
→ That's WHY you're stuck in Q1/Q3. Make time for Q2 to break the cycle.

## Summary: Live in Q2

**Key principles:**
1. **Most people live in Q1 and Q3** (crisis + noise)
2. **Effective people live in Q2** (strategic + proactive)
3. **Q2 prevents Q1** (planning prevents crises)
4. **Q3 is a trap** (urgent but not important = other people's priorities)
5. **Schedule Q2, protect Q2, track Q2**

**Goal:** 50%+ of your time in Q2.

**When you prioritize Q2, everything else gets easier.**
