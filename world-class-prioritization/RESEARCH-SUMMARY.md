## Research Coverage

### 1. Professional Prioritization Frameworks

**Sources Researched:**
- Product School, Monday.com, Delibr, Canny (product prioritization)
- PPM Express, LogRocket, GeeksforGeeks (prioritization techniques)
- AltExSoft, Ducalis (framework comparisons)

**Key Frameworks Identified:**
- **RICE** (Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort) - Intercom, for data-driven decisions
- **ICE** (Impact × Confidence × Ease) - Faster, MVP-focused version
- **MoSCoW** (Must/Should/Could/Won't) - Dai Clegg, for stakeholder alignment
- **WSJF** (Weighted Shortest Job First) - SAFe framework, prioritizes by Cost of Delay
- **CD3** (Cost of Delay ÷ Duration) - Economic prioritization
- **Kano Model** - Customer satisfaction-based (Basic/Performance/Delight features)
- **Value vs. Effort Matrix** - Simple visual 2×2
- **Buy a Feature** - Luke Hohmann's collaborative prioritization game

**Key Insights:**
- WSJF best for comprehensive coverage but time-intensive
- ICE/MoSCoW better for speed and simplicity
- Kano uniquely focused on customer satisfaction evolution
- Framework choice depends on context, data availability, and stakeholder needs

### 3. Delegation Strategies

**Sources Researched:**
- Asana, Harvard Business School, MIT Sloan (delegation effectiveness)
- Kissflow, ProProfs, Meister (delegation tactics)
- Eubrics, Join Genius (2025 delegation examples)

**Key Models:**

**Critical Success Factors:**
- Clear expectations (Context, Outcome, Authority, Resources, Timeline)
- Matching responsibility level to employee expertise
- Follow-up without micromanaging
- Trust but verify (especially early)

### 5. Time Blocking & Deep Work

**Sources Researched:**
- Cal Newport's official site, books, and planner
- NeuYear, Make Headway, Akiflow (Deep Work methodology)
- Accountability Muse (planner reviews)

**Cal Newport's Method:**
- Plan every minute of work day in blocks
- Deep work = cognitively demanding, creates value, hard to replicate
- Shallow work = necessary but not sufficient for success
- Time blockers accomplish ~2× more per week

**Implementation:**
- Minimum 90-minute blocks for deep work
- Protect with calendar holds
- Batch shallow work (email 3× per day, not constant)
- Re-block when plans change (flexibility within structure)

### 6. Energy Management & Chronotypes

**Sources Researched:**
- Vengreso, Udemy, Asrify (productivity trends 2025)
- Rolling Out (energy tracking)
- Zapier, DoorDash, PSD Challenge (chronotype research)
- Acacia Learning, 7 Minute Life (energy management)

**Major Paradigm Shift:**
- 2025 emphasizes **energy management** over time management
- "Energy, not time, is the currency that really matters"
- Time is fixed; energy is renewable and variable

**Chronotypes (4 Types):**
- **Lions** (15-20%): Peak 8am-12pm
- **Bears** (50%): Peak 10am-2pm (most common, 9-5 aligned)
- **Wolves** (15-20%): Peak 5pm-midnight (night owls)
- **Dolphins** (10%): Peak mid-morning to early afternoon

**Key Insight:**
- Working against your chronotype reduces effectiveness by 50%+
- Align most important tasks with Biological Prime Time
- Post-lunch dip (1-3pm) is universal—schedule low-cognitive tasks then

### 7. Decision Fatigue & Cognitive Load

**Sources Researched:**
- Journal of Neuroscience June 2025 study (neurobiology of cognitive fatigue)
- The Decision Lab, MasterClass (decision fatigue)
- Taylor's University, Rolling Out, ADHD Resource Center (2025 research)

**2025 Neuroscience Finding:**
- Adults make ~35,000 decisions daily
- Cognitive fatigue impacts dorsolateral prefrontal cortex
- When fatigued, people choose lower effort even for lower reward
- Brain = 2% body weight but 20% energy consumption

**Strategies:**
- Make important decisions during peak cognitive hours (early in day)
- Eliminate unnecessary decisions (routines, default rules)
- Batch similar decisions
- Delegate when appropriate
- Famous example: Obama/Jobs same outfit daily

### 8. Cost of Delay & WSJF

**Sources Researched:**
- Zeda.io, Next Product Group, Planview (Cost of Delay)
- Businessmap, Small Business Programming (CD3)
- Kanban Zone, FasterCapital (calculation methods)

**Framework:**
- **CoD** = User Value + Time Criticality + Risk Reduction/Opportunity Enablement
- **WSJF** = CoD ÷ Job Size
- **CD3** = Revenue potential ÷ (Months × 1000)

**Application:**
- Helps quantify "what's the cost of NOT doing this?"
- Shifts conversation from opinions to economics
- Highest WSJF = best ROI per unit of time

### 9. GTD (Getting Things Done)

**Sources Researched:**
- David Allen's official GTD site
- Float, Asana, Asian Efficiency (GTD guides 2025)
- Flow-E, Appfluence, Thomas Frank (implementation)

**The 5 Steps:**
1. **Capture** - Everything into trusted system
2. **Clarify** - Is it actionable? What's next action?
3. **Organize** - Lists by context
4. **Reflect** - Weekly review
5. **Engage** - Choose based on context, time, energy, priority

**Weekly Review** = Most Critical Habit:
- 15-25 minutes
- Get Clear → Get Current → Get Creative
- Prevents things from falling through cracks

### 12. Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)

**Sources Researched:**
- Asana, Reclaim, Doodle (Pareto for prioritization)
- FasterCapital, Active Collab, Project Wizards (applications)
- Interaction Design Foundation, MasterClass (80/20 explained)

**Universal Application:**
- 80% of results from 20% of efforts
- 80% of revenue from 20% of customers
- 80% of product value from 20% of features
- 80% of impact from 20% of tasks

**Prioritization Process:**
1. List all items
2. Measure impact
3. Rank by impact
4. Identify top 20%
5. Double down on top 20%, minimize bottom 80%

### 17. Ivy Lee Method

**Sources Researched:**
- ClickUp, Workflowy, Prialto (Ivy Lee implementation)
- Tweek, James Clear, Calendar.com (methodology)
- NIFS, Larksuite (applications)

**The Method (1918):**
1. Write down 6 most important tasks each evening
2. Prioritize them 1-6
3. Next day, work sequentially (finish #1 before starting #2)
4. Move unfinished tasks to tomorrow's list

**Why It Works:**
- Reduces decision fatigue (planned night before)
- Forces prioritization (only 6)
- Prevents multitasking (sequential)
- Charles Schwab paid $25,000 for this advice (=$400K today)

## Key Themes Across All Research

### 1. Shift from Time to Energy Management
Multiple 2025 sources emphasize that energy, not time, is the limiting factor. Managing when you do things (chronotype alignment) matters as much as what you do.

### 2. Data-Driven > Opinion-Driven
Professional prioritization increasingly emphasizes frameworks (RICE, WSJF) that use data to reduce political decision-making.

### 3. Values as Foundation
Personal prioritization consistently emphasizes values clarification as prerequisite to all other prioritization work.

### 4. Regular Review is Non-Negotiable
Every system (GTD, OKRs, Quarterly Planning) emphasizes review cycles. Without reflection, priorities drift.

### 5. Saying No is Strategic
Boundary-setting and saying no are increasingly recognized as core prioritization skills, not just soft skills.

### 6. Framework Selection Matters
No one-size-fits-all. Different decisions require different frameworks. Knowing when to use what is meta-skill.

### 7. Energy Renewal is Performance Fuel
2025 research emphasizes that rest, sleep, and recovery are not wasted time but essential for sustained high performance.

### 8. Cognitive Load Awareness
Decision fatigue research shows that the number and timing of decisions significantly impacts quality. Reducing unnecessary decisions preserves mental energy for important ones.
