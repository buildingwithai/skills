# Cinematography Technical Reference

## Complete Guide to Camera Language for Sora Prompts

This reference provides all cinematography terminology you need for detailed Sora prompts. Every term here can be used directly in your prompts.

## Camera Shot Types (Framing)

### By Subject Framing

**Extreme Close-Up (ECU / XCU)**
- **What it shows:** Eyes only, mouth only, hand detail, tiny object
- **When to use:** Intense emotion, important detail, intimacy
- **Emotional effect:** Extreme intimacy, discomfort, focus
- **Sora prompt:** "Extreme close-up of..."
- **Example:** "Extreme close-up of person's eye, pupil dilating"

**Close-Up (CU)**
- **What it shows:** Full face, head fills frame
- **When to use:** Emotion, reaction, performance, connection with viewer
- **Emotional effect:** Intimacy, emotion, character focus
- **Sora prompt:** "Close-up of..."
- **Example:** "Close-up of singer's face, eyes closed in emotion"

**Medium Close-Up (MCU)**
- **What it shows:** Head and shoulders
- **When to use:** Conversation, performance, showing expression + upper body
- **Emotional effect:** Personal but not extreme, comfortable distance
- **Sora prompt:** "Medium close-up of..."
- **Example:** "Medium close-up of person from chest up"

**Medium Shot (MS / Mid-Shot)**
- **What it shows:** Waist up, half body
- **When to use:** Performance, showing gestures, balanced framing
- **Emotional effect:** Neutral, informative, balanced
- **Sora prompt:** "Medium shot of..."
- **Example:** "Medium shot of guitarist from waist up, playing instrument"

**Medium Wide Shot (MWS / Cowboy Shot)**
- **What it shows:** Knees up, most of body
- **When to use:** Full body language, movement, action
- **Emotional effect:** Active, engaged, showing full presence
- **Sora prompt:** "Medium wide shot of..." or "Cowboy shot of..."
- **Example:** "Medium wide shot of dancer, body from knees up visible"

**Wide Shot (WS / Full Shot)**
- **What it shows:** Full body head to toe, subject in environment
- **When to use:** Establishing, showing location, full choreography
- **Emotional effect:** Context, scope, subject in world
- **Sora prompt:** "Wide shot of..."
- **Example:** "Wide shot of person standing in desert, full body visible"

**Extreme Wide Shot (EWS / Very Wide Shot)**
- **What it shows:** Subject small in vast environment
- **When to use:** Emphasize scale, isolation, epic scope
- **Emotional effect:** Isolation, scale, insignificance or triumph
- **Sora prompt:** "Extreme wide shot of..." or "Very wide shot of..."
- **Example:** "Extreme wide shot of tiny figure in massive cathedral"

### By Relationship/Perspective

**Over-the-Shoulder (OTS)**
- **What it shows:** From behind one subject looking at another
- **When to use:** Conversation, connection between two subjects
- **Emotional effect:** Voyeuristic, involved but observing
- **Sora prompt:** "Over-the-shoulder shot of..."
- **Example:** "Over-the-shoulder shot from behind person A looking at person B"

**Point of View (POV)**
- **What it shows:** What the subject sees through their eyes
- **When to use:** Subjective experience, immersion, first-person
- **Emotional effect:** Empathy, immersion, subjective
- **Sora prompt:** "POV shot..." or "First-person view..."
- **Example:** "POV shot of hands reaching forward toward door"

**Two-Shot**
- **What it shows:** Two subjects in frame together
- **When to use:** Relationship, connection, conversation
- **Emotional effect:** Relationship dynamics, connection/distance
- **Sora prompt:** "Two-shot of..."
- **Example:** "Two-shot of couple sitting on bench, space between them"

**Three-Shot / Group Shot**
- **What it shows:** Three or more subjects
- **When to use:** Group dynamics, ensemble
- **Emotional effect:** Community, isolation within group, dynamics
- **Sora prompt:** "Three-shot of..." or "Group shot of..."

**Insert Shot**
- **What it shows:** Close detail of object or action
- **When to use:** Show important detail, symbolic object
- **Emotional effect:** Focus attention, symbolic importance
- **Sora prompt:** "Insert shot of..."
- **Example:** "Insert shot of hand turning doorknob"

## Camera Angles (Vertical Position)

**Eye Level**
- **Camera position:** At subject's eye height
- **Effect:** Neutral, equality, natural
- **When to use:** Standard, realistic perspective
- **Sora prompt:** "Eye-level camera..." or (default - don't specify)
- **Example:** "Eye-level medium shot of person speaking"

**High Angle (Looking Down)**
- **Camera position:** Above subject looking down
- **Effect:** Subject appears weak, vulnerable, small, overwhelmed
- **When to use:** Showing vulnerability, defeat, innocence
- **Sora prompt:** "High-angle shot..." or "Camera looking down at..."
- **Example:** "High-angle shot of person curled on floor, appears small and vulnerable"

**Low Angle (Looking Up)**
- **Camera position:** Below subject looking up
- **Effect:** Subject appears powerful, dominant, threatening, heroic
- **When to use:** Power, authority, intimidation, triumph
- **Sora prompt:** "Low-angle shot..." or "Camera looking up at..."
- **Example:** "Low-angle shot of person standing triumphant, appears powerful against sky"

**Bird's Eye View (Overhead)**
- **Camera position:** Directly overhead looking straight down
- **Effect:** God's perspective, pattern, disorientation, abstraction
- **When to use:** Unique perspective, showing patterns, geography
- **Sora prompt:** "Bird's eye view..." or "Overhead shot..."
- **Example:** "Bird's eye view of person lying in field of flowers, creating mandala pattern"

**Dutch Angle (Tilted Frame)**
- **Camera position:** Tilted on axis (horizon is diagonal)
- **Effect:** Unease, disorientation, chaos, instability, dynamic energy
- **When to use:** Tension, madness, dynamic energy, unease
- **Sora prompt:** "Dutch angle shot..." or "Tilted frame..."
- **Example:** "Dutch angle medium shot, frame tilted 30 degrees, creating unease"

**Worm's Eye View (Ground Level)**
- **Camera position:** At ground level looking up
- **Effect:** Extreme powerlessness or extreme power, surreal
- **When to use:** Unique perspective, extreme emotion
- **Sora prompt:** "Worm's eye view..."
- **Example:** "Worm's eye view from ground as person walks past, towering above camera"

## Camera Movements

**Static / Locked-Off**
- **Movement:** No movement, tripod-stable
- **Effect:** Stability, observation, stillness, control
- **When to use:** Emphasize stillness, stability, let subject move
- **Sora prompt:** "Static camera" or "Locked-off camera" or "No camera movement"
- **Example:** "Static camera, locked-off frame, subject moves while camera stays still"

**Pan (Horizontal Rotation)**
- **Movement:** Camera rotates left or right on fixed point
- **Effect:** Follow subject, reveal space, connect elements
- **When to use:** Track moving subject, reveal environment
- **Sora prompt:** "Camera pans left/right" or "Slow pan..." or "Whip pan..."
- **Example:** "Slow pan right following person walking across frame"

**Tilt (Vertical Rotation)**
- **Movement:** Camera rotates up or down on fixed point
- **Effect:** Reveal height, scale, connection
- **When to use:** Show tall objects, sky to ground, scale
- **Sora prompt:** "Camera tilts up/down"
- **Example:** "Camera tilts up from ground to reveal towering building"

**Dolly / Track (Push In / Pull Out)**
- **Movement:** Camera physically moves forward or backward
- **Effect:** Approach (intimacy, focus) or retreat (context, distance)
- **When to use:** Build intimacy, create space, follow movement
- **Sora prompt:** "Slow dolly in" or "Pull back" or "Push in toward..."
- **Example:** "Slow dolly push in from wide shot to close-up over full duration"

**Truck / Tracking (Lateral Movement)**
- **Movement:** Camera moves parallel to subject (left/right)
- **Effect:** Follow action, show environment, dynamic
- **When to use:** Track walking, show parallel movement
- **Sora prompt:** "Camera trucks left/right" or "Tracking shot..."
- **Example:** "Camera trucks right alongside person walking, matching their pace"

**Crane / Jib (Vertical Movement)**
- **Movement:** Camera raises or lowers on crane/jib arm
- **Effect:** Reveal scope, shift perspective, grand movement
- **When to use:** Epic reveals, changing scale, establishing
- **Sora prompt:** "Crane up/down" or "Camera rises/descends"
- **Example:** "Slow crane up from ground level to 30 feet, revealing full landscape"

**Zoom (Lens Focal Length Change)**
- **Movement:** Lens zooms in or out (perspective changes slightly)
- **Effect:** Attention focus (zoom in) or context (zoom out)
- **When to use:** Quick framing change, attention shift
- **Sora prompt:** "Zoom in/out" or "Slow zoom toward..."
- **Example:** "Slow zoom in on person's face as realization dawns"
- **Note:** Different from dolly - perspective doesn't change the same way

**Steadicam / Gimbal (Smooth Handheld)**
- **Movement:** Smooth floating movement, follows subject fluidly
- **Effect:** Immersive, following, energetic but smooth
- **When to use:** Following subjects, smooth dynamic movement
- **Sora prompt:** "Steadicam shot" or "Smooth gimbal movement" or "Floating camera following..."
- **Example:** "Steadicam shot smoothly following person through crowd"

**Handheld (Organic Shakiness)**
- **Movement:** Visible camera shake, human operator feel
- **Effect:** Realism, rawness, documentary feel, energy or chaos
- **When to use:** Raw emotion, documentary style, chaos, intimacy
- **Sora prompt:** "Handheld camera" or "Shaky camera"
- **Example:** "Handheld camera with organic movement, creating raw intimate feel"

**Orbit / Arc (Circular Movement)**
- **Movement:** Camera circles around subject
- **Effect:** Revealing, dynamic, showcasing, examination
- **When to use:** Showcase subject, reveal environment, dynamic energy
- **Sora prompt:** "Camera orbits around..." or "360-degree orbit" or "Arc around..."
- **Example:** "Slow 180-degree orbit around subject from left to right"

**Whip Pan (Fast Blur)**
- **Movement:** Extremely fast pan creating motion blur
- **Effect:** Transition, energy, surprise, disorientation
- **When to use:** Transitions between scenes, high energy moments
- **Sora prompt:** "Whip pan left/right"
- **Example:** "Whip pan right creating motion blur transition"

**Vertigo Effect / Dolly Zoom**
- **Movement:** Dolly in while zooming out (or vice versa)
- **Effect:** Disorientation, realization, unease, surreal
- **When to use:** Psychological moments, realization, unease
- **Sora prompt:** "Dolly zoom effect" or "Vertigo effect"
- **Example:** "Dolly zoom: camera pulls back while zooming in, background warps"
- **Note:** Complex for AI - may not execute perfectly

## Lens Choices & Focal Lengths

**Wide-Angle Lens / 24mm or less**
- **Effect:** Expansive view, slight distortion, deep focus, immersive
- **Perspective:** Exaggerated depth, foreground larger
- **When to use:** Cramped spaces feel larger, dynamic movement, environments
- **Sora prompt:** "24mm wide-angle lens" or "Wide-angle lens"
- **Example:** "24mm wide-angle lens creating expansive view with slight distortion"

**Standard Lens / 35mm**
- **Effect:** Natural, cinematic, balanced, most film-like
- **Perspective:** Similar to human eye, cinematic standard
- **When to use:** Most situations, natural feel, professional look
- **Sora prompt:** "35mm lens"
- **Example:** "35mm lens creating natural cinematic perspective"

**Normal Lens / 50mm**
- **Effect:** Most like human vision, neutral, intimate
- **Perspective:** No distortion, natural proportions
- **When to use:** Portrait, natural perspective, realism
- **Sora prompt:** "50mm lens"
- **Example:** "50mm lens with natural human-eye perspective"

**Telephoto Lens / 85mm-135mm**
- **Effect:** Compressed perspective, shallow depth of field, flattering portraits
- **Perspective:** Background appears closer, compressed
- **When to use:** Portraits, isolate subject, compress space
- **Sora prompt:** "85mm telephoto lens" or "Telephoto lens"
- **Example:** "85mm telephoto lens with compressed perspective, subject isolated from background"

**Long Telephoto / 200mm+**
- **Effect:** Extreme compression, very shallow DOF, abstract backgrounds
- **Perspective:** Extreme compression, flat appearance
- **When to use:** Extreme subject isolation, voyeuristic feel
- **Sora prompt:** "Long telephoto lens" or "200mm lens"

**Fisheye Lens**
- **Effect:** Extreme spherical distortion, ultra-wide view, surreal
- **Perspective:** Warped, spherical, edges distorted
- **When to use:** Surreal looks, unique perspective, music video style
- **Sora prompt:** "Fisheye lens" or "Fisheye distortion"
- **Example:** "Fisheye lens creating extreme spherical distortion and surreal warping"

**Macro Lens**
- **Effect:** Extreme close-up detail, shallow DOF
- **Perspective:** Tiny objects huge, abstract detail
- **When to use:** Details, texture, abstract beauty
- **Sora prompt:** "Macro lens" or "Extreme macro close-up"
- **Example:** "Macro lens extreme close-up of water droplet on leaf"

**Anamorphic Lens**
- **Effect:** Oval bokeh, horizontal lens flare, widescreen aspect, cinematic
- **Perspective:** 2.39:1 widescreen look
- **When to use:** Premium cinematic feel, horizontal flares
- **Sora prompt:** "Anamorphic lens" or "Anamorphic widescreen"
- **Example:** "35mm anamorphic lens with characteristic horizontal lens flare and oval bokeh"

## Depth of Field

**Shallow Depth of Field (Shallow DOF)**
- **What it means:** Subject sharp, background blurred (bokeh)
- **How achieved:** Wide aperture (f/1.4, f/2.8), telephoto lens
- **Effect:** Subject isolation, dreamy, cinematic, focus attention
- **When to use:** Isolate subject, create depth, cinematic feel
- **Sora prompt:** "Shallow depth of field" or "Blurred background"
- **Example:** "50mm lens, shallow depth of field, subject sharp with blurred dreamy background"

**Deep Focus / Deep Depth of Field**
- **What it means:** Everything sharp from foreground to background
- **How achieved:** Small aperture (f/11, f/16), wide lens
- **Effect:** Documentary feel, Wes Anderson style, clarity throughout
- **When to use:** Everything important, stylized, documentary
- **Sora prompt:** "Deep focus" or "Deep depth of field" or "Everything in sharp focus"
- **Example:** "Wide-angle lens, deep focus, foreground and background both sharp"

## Film Formats & Aspect Ratios

**35mm Film**
- **Look:** Classic cinematic, slight grain, warm
- **When to use:** Standard cinematic feel
- **Sora prompt:** "35mm film" or "Shot on 35mm"

**16mm Film**
- **Look:** Grainier, indie feel, documentary
- **When to use:** Indie aesthetic, nostalgic
- **Sora prompt:** "16mm film" or "16mm grain texture"

**Super 8 Film**
- **Look:** Very grainy, vintage, nostalgic, home movie
- **When to use:** Vintage 70s/80s feel, memories
- **Sora prompt:** "Super 8 film" or "Super 8 vintage home movie aesthetic"

**70mm IMAX**
- **Look:** Ultra-crisp, epic scale, premium
- **When to use:** Epic moments, maximum quality
- **Sora prompt:** "70mm IMAX" or "IMAX format"

**Digital / Clean**
- **Look:** Crisp, modern, clean, no grain
- **When to use:** Modern look, clarity
- **Sora prompt:** "Digital cinema camera" or (default - often don't specify)

## Composition Techniques

**Rule of Thirds**
- **Technique:** Divide frame into 3x3 grid, place subject on intersections
- **Effect:** Balanced, pleasing, professional
- **Sora prompt:** "Composed using rule of thirds" or "Subject placed on third line"

**Center Framing / Symmetry**
- **Technique:** Subject dead center, often symmetrical
- **Effect:** Formal, powerful, Wes Anderson style, confrontational
- **Sora prompt:** "Centered framing" or "Perfect symmetry"

**Leading Lines**
- **Technique:** Use lines in scene to lead eye to subject
- **Effect:** Guides attention, depth, dynamic
- **Sora prompt:** "Leading lines drawing eye to subject"

**Negative Space**
- **Technique:** Lot of empty space around subject
- **Effect:** Isolation, minimalism, emphasis on subject
- **Sora prompt:** "Significant negative space" or "Minimalist composition with negative space"

**Foreground Framing**
- **Technique:** Frame subject through foreground objects
- **Effect:** Depth, voyeurism, layers
- **Sora prompt:** "Framed through foreground elements"

**Fill the Frame**
- **Technique:** Subject fills entire frame tightly
- **Effect:** Intimacy, intensity, claustrophobia
- **Sora prompt:** "Filling the frame" or "Tight framing"

## Combined Prompt Examples

**Example 1: Intimate Performance**
```
Medium close-up of singer; 50mm lens, shallow depth of field; static camera, no movement; soft key light from 45 degrees creating gentle shadow; warm color grade; composed using rule of thirds with subject slightly off-center
```

**Example 2: Epic Isolation**
```
Extreme wide shot of tiny figure; 24mm wide-angle lens, deep focus; slow crane up from ground level to 40 feet high; natural overcast lighting; desaturated cool color palette; centered symmetrical framing emphasizing isolation
```

**Example 3: Dynamic Energy**
```
Wide shot; 35mm lens; handheld camera with energetic movement following subject; harsh overhead practical lighting; high-contrast saturated colors; Dutch angle tilted 20 degrees creating dynamic instability
```

**Example 4: Surreal Dreamlike**
```
Medium shot; 85mm telephoto lens, extremely shallow depth of field; slow dolly zoom effect; volumetric lighting creating visible light rays; pastel dream-like color palette; centered framing with bokeh background
```

## Quick Reference: Emotion to Cinematography

**For INTIMACY:** Close-up, 50mm, shallow DOF, soft lighting, static camera
**For POWER:** Low angle, wide lens, deep focus, dramatic lighting
**For ISOLATION:** Extreme wide, centered subject, negative space
**For CHAOS:** Handheld, Dutch angle, fast movement, harsh lighting
**For DREAMINESS:** Shallow DOF, slow movement, soft lighting, pastel colors
**For RAWNESS:** Handheld, 16mm film look, natural lighting, close framing
**For EPIC SCALE:** Extreme wide, crane movement, 35mm or 70mm, golden hour
