## Key Principles

### 1. Positioning and Messaging Are Non-Negotiable

You cannot launch successfully without:
- Clear positioning (who you're for, why you're different)
- Validated messaging (value propositions that resonate)

**95% of products fail due to insufficient market understanding.**

### 2. Launch Tiers Drive Resource Allocation

Not all launches deserve equal investment:
- **Tier 1:** Maximum 2 per quarter, full resources
- **Tier 2:** 4-8 per quarter, selective resources
- **Tier 3:** Ongoing, minimal resources

### 3. Test Everything Before Launch Day

Assumptions are dangerous:
- Beta test with real users
- Validate messaging with Wynter/UserTesting (use `rollout-and-launch` skill)
- Test pricing psychology
- Validate onboarding friction

### 4. The First 48 Hours Matter Most

Launch day execution requires:
- War room intensity
- Hour-by-hour playbook
- Real-time monitoring
- Crisis protocols ready

### 5. Post-Launch is Where Real Work Begins

Week 1 feedback reveals what months of planning missed:
- 2-week sprint cycles
- Continuous deployment
- Tight feedback loops
- AARRR metrics tracking
