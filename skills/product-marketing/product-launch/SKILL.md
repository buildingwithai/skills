## Launch Types & Playbooks

### Tier 1: Major Product Launch ("Scream")
**Definition:** New product, new market entry, major repositioning, acquisition launch
**Frequency:** Maximum 2 per quarter
**Resources:** Full cross-functional team, significant budget
**Timeline:** 16-24 weeks
**Use:** Full framework above, all phases

### Tier 2: Feature Launch ("Shout")
**Definition:** Significant feature, retention driver, revenue unlock
**Frequency:** 4-8 per quarter
**Resources:** Core product marketing, selective sales enablement
**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks
**Use:** 03-launch-tier ‚Üí 07-beta ‚Üí 10-sales-enablement ‚Üí 12-community ‚Üí 16-metrics

### Tier 3: Incremental Update ("Whisper")
**Definition:** Maintenance releases, minor improvements, bug fixes
**Frequency:** Ongoing
**Resources:** PM + support documentation
**Timeline:** 1-4 weeks
**Use:** Release notes, in-app notifications, basic metrics

## Quick Decision Trees

### "What tier is my launch?"

**Ask:**
1. Does this acquire NEW customers or open NEW markets? ‚Üí YES = Tier 1 candidate
2. Does this impact REVENUE or significantly improve RETENTION? ‚Üí YES = Tier 2 candidate
3. Is this maintenance, bug fixes, or minor improvements? ‚Üí YES = Tier 3

**Note:** Development effort ‚â† launch tier. Impact to market/business determines tier.

### "What's my GTM motion?"

**Product-Led Growth (PLG):**
- Low price point or freemium
- Simple onboarding (<15 minutes to value)
- Self-serve model
- Viral mechanisms
- Examples: Slack, Notion, Figma

**Sales-Led Growth:**
- High ACV (>$50K)
- Complex implementation
- Custom pricing/contracts
- Multi-stakeholder decisions
- Examples: Salesforce, SAP, enterprise software

**Hybrid (Most Common 2024):**
- Free trial ‚Üí paid conversion
- PLG for SMB, sales for enterprise
- Land with users, expand through economic buyer
- Examples: Atlassian, HubSpot, Monday.com

### "What channels should I prioritize?"

**B2B SaaS:**

## Key Principles (The Foundation of 1% Launches)

### 1. Customer Discovery Before Building
**Principle:** Validate problem and solution before investing in product.
**Why it matters:** 95% of products fail due to lack of market understanding.
**How to apply:** 8-10 customer interviews, jobs-to-be-done research, landing page validation.

### 2. Positioning Precedes Messaging
**Principle:** You must know WHO you serve, WHAT makes you different, and WHY it matters before writing a single word of copy.
**Framework:** April Dunford's 5 components (best-fit customers, alternatives, differentiation, value, market category).
**Application:** Complete positioning workshop before any launch materials.

### 3. Launch Tiers Drive Resource Allocation
**Principle:** Not all launches deserve equal investment. Tier 1 launches are rare but critical.
**Reality check:** Marketing teams can only handle 2 Tier 1 launches per quarter maximum.
**Decision criteria:** Market/business impact, NOT development effort.

### 4. Test Everything Before Launch Day
**Principle:** Assumptions are dangerous. Validate with real users.
**What to test:** Value proposition clarity, messaging resonance, pricing psychology, onboarding friction.
**Methods:** Wynter message testing, UserTesting, sales pitch tests, beta programs.

### 5. Cross-Functional Alignment is Non-Negotiable
**Principle:** A launch is only as good as its weakest functional handoff.
**RACI application:** Define who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed for every launch activity.
**Communication:** Weekly all-hands during pre-launch, daily standups final 2 weeks.

### 6. The First 48 Hours Make or Break Momentum
**Principle:** Launch day execution requires war room intensity and real-time response.
**Preparation:** Hour-by-hour playbook, designated responders, monitoring dashboards, crisis protocols.
**2024 reality:** News travels 6x faster online - be ready for rapid response.

### 7. Product-Led Growth Wins in 2024
**Principle:** Let the product sell itself. PLG companies grow 2.4x faster than sales-led.
**Core elements:** Fast time-to-value (<15 min), clear Aha! moment, viral loops, self-serve model.
**Trend:** 75% of B2B buyers prefer rep-free experiences.

### 8. Metrics Drive Decisions, Not Opinions
**Principle:** AARRR framework (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue) guides optimization.
**Key metrics:** CAC, LTV, activation rate, retention curves, NPS, MRR/ARR.
**Discipline:** A/B test relentlessly. Statistical significance > gut feel.

### 9. Post-Launch is Where Real Work Begins
**Principle:** Launch is a starting line, not a finish line.
**Reality:** Week 1 feedback reveals what months of planning missed.
**Iteration:** 2-week sprint cycles, continuous deployment, feedback loops.

### 10. Community Beats Advertising
**Principle:** Authentic engagement on Product Hunt, Reddit, IndieHackers generates better ROI than paid ads for early-stage launches.
**Authenticity requirement:** 2024 communities punish promotional content. Provide value first.
**Long game:** Start building community 3-6 months before launch.

### 11. Video Content Dominates (2024 Trend)
**Principle:** Short-form video views on LinkedIn increased 6x in 2024.
**Application:** Product demos, founder stories, customer testimonials, feature highlights.
**Platforms:** LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok (B2C), Instagram Reels.

### 12. Pricing Anchors Perception Forever
**Principle:** Your launch price creates permanent perception. Raising later is hard.
**Psychology:** If you anchor too low, you may never escape that gravity.
**Strategy:** Value-based pricing, Good-Better-Best tiers, test before public launch.

### 13. AI Levels the Playing Field for Solopreneurs
**Principle:** 2024 GenAI enables one-person launches that rival small teams.
**Applications:** Content creation, image generation, customer support, analytics.
**Reality:** 42% of solopreneurs prioritize revenue over new launches - AI helps do more with less.

### 14. Crisis Preparedness Prevents Disasters
**Principle:** 51% of companies lack crisis communication plans, yet crises are inevitable.
**Preparation:** Pre-written response templates, designated spokesperson, escalation paths.
**Speed matters:** First response should be within 1 hour of issue identification.

### 15. Learn from Failure (Most Products Fail)
**Principle:** 95% failure rate means learning from mistakes is competitive advantage.
**Common failures:** Poor pricing, weak differentiation, insufficient market research, bad timing, feature bloat.
**Response:** Fast pivots, customer feedback integration, willingness to kill bad ideas.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid (Lessons from 2024 Failures)

### Strategic Mistakes

1. **Insufficient Market Research**
   - **Impact:** #1 reason for 95% failure rate
   - **Solution:** 8-10 customer discovery interviews, JTBD research, competitive analysis

2. **Weak Value Proposition**
   - **Impact:** Prospects don't understand why they should care
   - **Solution:** Test messaging on Wynter, 30-second pitch test, mom test

3. **Wrong Pricing**
   - **Impact:** Can kill an otherwise great product
   - **Solution:** Value-based pricing research, A/B testing, Good-Better-Best tiers

4. **Launching with Missing Features**
   - **Example:** Threads failed despite Meta's resources due to incomplete MVP
   - **Solution:** Core value must work perfectly, nice-to-haves can wait

5. **Poor Differentiation**
   - **Impact:** Lost in sea of competitors
   - **Solution:** Category creation vs competition, positioning workshops, unique capabilities

### Execution Mistakes

6. **No Launch Tier Framework**
   - **Impact:** Every launch gets maximum resources, team burnout
   - **Solution:** Implement Tier 1/2/3 framework, match resources to impact

7. **Skipping Beta Testing**
   - **Impact:** Major bugs and usability issues on launch day
   - **Solution:** 3-4 week closed beta minimum, feedback loops

8. **Inadequate Sales Enablement**
   - **Impact:** Sales team can't articulate value, deals stall
   - **Solution:** Battlecards, training sessions, discovery questions, demo environments

9. **No Post-Launch Plan**
   - **Impact:** Momentum dies after launch day
   - **Solution:** 90-day growth plan, iteration cycles, continuous optimization

10. **Missing Cross-Functional Alignment**
    - **Impact:** Product/Marketing/Sales/CS have different stories
    - **Solution:** RACI matrix, weekly syncs, single source of truth

### Marketing Mistakes

11. **One-Size-Fits-All Messaging**
    - **Impact:** Doesn't resonate with any audience
    - **Solution:** Persona-specific messaging (technical buyers, economic buyers, users)

12. **Weak Pre-Launch Audience Building**
    - **Impact:** No one shows up on launch day
    - **Solution:** 4-8 week pre-launch, waitlist building, community engagement

13. **Ignoring SEO Before Launch**
    - **Impact:** Organic traffic takes months to build
    - **Solution:** Publish content 4-6 weeks before launch for indexing

14. **Over-Reliance on Paid Advertising**
    - **Impact:** Expensive, not sustainable for early-stage
    - **Solution:** Community-first (Product Hunt, Reddit), organic content

15. **No Crisis Communication Plan**
    - **Impact:** Scrambling during launch day issues
    - **Solution:** Pre-written templates, war room protocols, designated responders

### Measurement Mistakes

16. **Vanity Metrics Over Real Metrics**
    - **Impact:** Chasing social media followers instead of revenue
    - **Solution:** AARRR framework, focus on activation and retention

17. **No Statistical Rigor**
    - **Impact:** Acting on noise rather than signal
    - **Solution:** Proper sample sizes, confidence intervals, A/B testing discipline

18. **Set and Forget**
    - **Impact:** Market changes, messaging becomes stale
    - **Solution:** Quarterly message testing, continuous iteration
