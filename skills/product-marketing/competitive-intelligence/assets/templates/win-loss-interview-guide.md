## Interview Script

### Introduction (2 minutes)

**Opening**:
> "Hi [Name], thank you so much for taking the time to speak with me today. I really appreciate it.
>
> My name is [Your Name], and I work in [Product Marketing/Competitive Intelligence] at [Your Company]. The purpose of this call is to learn from your evaluation process and experience to help us improve.
>
> This is not a sales call - your feedback will be kept confidential and aggregated with other interviews. We won't share individual responses with our sales team, though we may use anonymized themes.
>
> I'm planning for this to take about 30 minutes. Is that still good for you?
>
> [If recording] Do I have your permission to record this conversation for note-taking purposes? The recording is only for internal use and won't be shared externally."

**Set the Tone**:
> "I'm here to learn and listen. There are no right or wrong answers - I'm genuinely curious about your experience and want to understand your perspective. Please be as candid as possible; that's most helpful for us."

---

### Background (3-5 minutes)

**Role and Context**:
1. "Tell me a bit about your role and what led you to evaluate solutions in this space."
   - [Listen for: business problem, pain points, scope]

2. "What was the business problem or opportunity you were trying to address?"
   - [Listen for: use case, urgency, success criteria]

3. "Who was involved in the decision-making process?"
   - [Listen for: decision-makers, influencers, technical evaluators]
   - *Probe*: "How did different stakeholders prioritize requirements differently?"

---

### Evaluation Process (5-7 minutes)

**The Journey**:
4. "Walk me through your evaluation process from the very beginning - how did you first start looking at solutions?"
   - [Listen for: trigger event, research process, vendor discovery]

5. "Which vendors did you evaluate, and how did they get on your list?"
   - [Listen for: competitors, consideration set, shortlist criteria]
   - *Probe*: "Were there any vendors you looked at briefly but eliminated early? Why?"

6. "What were your key decision criteria?"
   - [Listen for: features, price, fit, support, trust, etc.]
   - *Probe*: "If you had to rank your top 3 criteria, what would they be?"

7. "How did you weight different factors - like price vs features vs ease of implementation?"
   - [Listen for: trade-off decisions, priorities]

8. "How long did your evaluation take from start to finish?"
   - [Listen for: sales cycle, any delays or bottlenecks]
   - *Probe*: "Was that faster or slower than you expected? Why?"

---

### Competitive Dynamics (8-12 minutes) - CRITICAL SECTION

**Vendor Comparison**:
9. "How did [Your Company] and [Competitor(s)] compare across your key criteria?"
   - [Listen for: head-to-head comparison, relative strengths/weaknesses]
   - *Make a mental or written note to ask about each competitor*

**For Each Competitor**:
10. "Let's talk about [Competitor X] specifically. What stood out as their key strengths?"
    - [Listen for: what they do well, compelling features, differentiators]

11. "Were there any concerns or weaknesses you identified with [Competitor X]?"
    - [Listen for: gaps, limitations, red flags]
    - *Probe*: "Can you give me a specific example?"

12. "How did our sales process and team compare to [Competitor X]'s?"
    - [Listen for: responsiveness, expertise, helpfulness, pressure]

13. "Did [Competitor X] say or do anything that influenced your decision significantly?"
    - [Listen for: competitive tactics, positioning, claims about you]
    - *Probe*: "Did they mention us or other vendors? What did they say?"

**Surprise and Differentiation**:
14. "What surprised you during the evaluation - either positively or negatively?"
    - [Listen for: unexpected discoveries, disappointments]

15. "Were there any 'make or break' factors or deal-breaker requirements?"
    - [Listen for: must-haves, non-negotiables]

---

### Decision Factors (5-7 minutes)

**The Final Decision**:

**IF WIN**:
16. "What were the top 3 reasons you ultimately chose [Your Company]?"
    - [Listen for: primary decision drivers - rank them]
    - *Probe*: "Can you rank those for me - which was most important?"

17. "Was there anything that nearly made you choose [Competitor] instead?"
    - [Listen for: close call factors, hesitations]

18. "How close was the final decision on a scale of 1-10? (1 = clear choice, 10 = agonizing)"
    - [Listen for: confidence level, clarity]
    - *Probe*: "What made it [clear/difficult]?"

**IF LOSS**:
16. "What were the top 3 reasons you chose [Competitor] over us?"
    - [Listen for: why we lost, their advantages]
    - *Probe*: "Can you rank those? Which was the deciding factor?"

17. "Was there anything we could have done differently to win your business?"
    - [Listen for: missed opportunities, what would have changed outcome]
    - *Probe*: "If we had [X capability/feature], would that have changed your decision?"

18. "How close was the decision? On a scale of 1-10, with 1 being 'clearly them' and 10 being 'agonizingly close'?"
    - [Listen for: how competitive it was]

**BOTH WIN & LOSS**:
19. "If you had to do it all over again, would you make the same decision?"
    - [Listen for: confidence, satisfaction, second thoughts]

---

### Product and Sales Feedback (5-7 minutes)

**Sales Experience**:
20. "How was your experience with our sales team and sales process?"
    - [Listen for: responsiveness, knowledge, consultative approach, pressure]
    - *Probe*: "Was there anything they did particularly well or could have improved?"

21. "How did our demo or proof of concept go? What worked well? What could have been better?"
    - [Listen for: demo effectiveness, POC value, technical evaluation]

**Product Feedback**:
22. "Were there any product features or capabilities you wish we had?"
    - [Listen for: feature gaps, unmet needs, wishlist]
    - *Probe*: "How important was that gap - would it have changed your decision?"

23. "What did you think about our product's [ease of use / implementation / integrations]?"
    - [Listen for: usability, technical fit, friction points]

**Pricing**:
24. "How did our pricing and packaging compare to your expectations and [Competitor]'s?"
    - [Listen for: price sensitivity, value perception, pricing model fit]
    - *Probe*: "Was price a major factor in your decision?"

**Materials and Content**:
25. "How helpful were our sales materials - like case studies, demos, proposals, battlecards?"
    - [Listen for: content effectiveness, gaps]

---

### Wrap-Up (2-3 minutes)

**Final Thoughts**:
26. "Is there anything else about your evaluation or decision that you think we should know?"
    - [Listen for: anything missed, final insights]

27. "Any final advice for us as we work to improve?"
    - [Listen for: actionable recommendations]

**Closing**:
> "Thank you so much for your time and candid feedback. This is incredibly valuable to us as we continue to improve.
>
> [If WIN] We're excited to partner with you, and I'll make sure this feedback gets to the right teams to ensure a great experience.
>
> [If LOSS] I really appreciate your honesty. Even though we didn't win this time, this insight helps us improve for the future. If circumstances change or we can help down the line, please don't hesitate to reach out.
>
> As a thank you, I'll send [incentive - gift card, etc.] to [email] within 24 hours.
>
> If you think of anything else, feel free to email me at [your email]. Thanks again!"

## Tips for Effective Interviews

**Do**:
- ✅ Ask open-ended questions
- ✅ Probe deeper with "Why?" and "Can you give an example?"
- ✅ Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- ✅ Stay neutral and non-defensive
- ✅ Thank them sincerely for their time
- ✅ Follow up promptly with incentive

**Don't**:
- ❌ Defend your product or argue
- ❌ Bash competitors
- ❌ Ask leading questions
- ❌ Rush through - let them talk
- ❌ Forget to probe on competitive dynamics
- ❌ Skip the incentive or delay sending it

**Probing Techniques**:
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "Why was that important to you?"
- "How did that compare to [other vendor]?"
- "What did you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Walk me through that..."

---

This guide is designed to extract maximum competitive intelligence while also gathering valuable product and sales feedback. The key is making interviewees comfortable and letting them tell their story.
