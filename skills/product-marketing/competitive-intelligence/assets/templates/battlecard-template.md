## Objection Handling

### Objection: "They're cheaper"

**Response**:
- **Acknowledge**: "I understand price is a consideration..."
- **Reframe**: "The real question is total cost of ownership over 3 years. When you factor in [implementation time, training, support costs, add-ons needed for full functionality], customers typically find..."
- **Prove**: "Here's a TCO analysis from [similar customer] showing they saved [X%] with us over 3 years despite higher list price."

---

### Objection: "They have [specific feature] we need"

**Response**:
- **Acknowledge**: "Yes, they do have [feature] in their platform..."
- **Reframe**: "The key question is how well it works in production and whether it delivers [business outcome]. We've found customers who tried their [feature] switched to us because..."
- **Prove**: "Let me show you how we deliver [same outcome] with [alternative approach] that's actually better because..."

---

### Objection: "They're more established / been around longer"

**Response**:
- **Acknowledge**: "They have been in the market since [year]..."
- **Reframe**: "The question is whether you want legacy technology built 10 years ago, or modern architecture designed for [current need]. Their age also means technical debt, monolithic systems, and slower innovation..."
- **Prove**: "Here are 5 customers who switched from them specifically because we're more innovative and agile: [examples]"
