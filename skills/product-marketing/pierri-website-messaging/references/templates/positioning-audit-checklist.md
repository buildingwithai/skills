# Positioning Audit Checklist

**Purpose**: Evaluate your current homepage positioning and identify areas for improvement
**Based on**: Anthony Pierri's positioning and messaging framework

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Visit your current homepage
2. Answer each question honestly (Yes/No/Partial)
3. Score each section
4. Identify priority improvements
5. Use specific guides to fix issues

**Scoring**:
- ✅ Yes = 2 points
- ⚠️ Partial = 1 point
- ❌ No = 0 points

---

## SECTION 1: Above-the-Fold Clarity (Hero Section)

### The Four Positioning Questions

**1.1 Can a visitor understand WHAT your product is in 5 seconds?**
- [ ] Yes - Category or use case is immediately clear
- [ ] Partial - Takes some interpretation
- [ ] No - Unclear what the product actually is

**Example of YES**: "The collaborative interface design tool"
**Example of NO**: "Transform the future of work"

---

**1.2 Is it clear WHO the product is for?**
- [ ] Yes - Target customer/segment is explicitly stated or clearly implied
- [ ] Partial - Somewhat clear but could be more specific
- [ ] No - Could be for anyone / too broad

**Example of YES**: "For design teams at tech companies"
**Example of NO**: "For innovative teams"

---

**1.3 Does the hero communicate what makes you DIFFERENT?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear, specific differentiation
- [ ] Partial - Implied but not explicit
- [ ] No - Generic claims or no differentiation

**Example of YES**: "Real-time collaboration" (when competitors didn't have it)
**Example of NO**: "Better user experience"

---

**1.4 Is it clear WHY it matters (value/benefit)?**
- [ ] Yes - Specific outcome or value stated
- [ ] Partial - Generic benefit mentioned
- [ ] No - No benefit communicated

**Example of YES**: "Ship features 2x faster"
**Example of NO**: "Drive growth"

---

### Hero Section Elements

**1.5 Is your H1 headline clear and compelling?**
- [ ] Yes - Immediately conveys what/who/different
- [ ] Partial - Conveys one or two elements
- [ ] No - Vague or confusing

---

**1.6 Does your H2 add helpful context?**
- [ ] Yes - Expands on H1, adds target audience or capability details
- [ ] Partial - Repeats H1 in different words
- [ ] No - Doesn't add value or is confusing

---

**1.7 Is your primary CTA clear and low-friction?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear action, low friction (free trial, demo, etc.)
- [ ] Partial - Clear but high friction
- [ ] No - Unclear or confusing

---

**1.8 Does your hero visual show the actual product?**
- [ ] Yes - Real product screenshot or demo
- [ ] Partial - Abstract illustration that relates to product
- [ ] No - Generic stock photo or unrelated visual

---

## SECTION 2: Target Audience & Positioning

**2.1 Have you chosen a specific target audience?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear segment (e.g., "Product designers at Series A-C SaaS companies")
- [ ] Partial - Somewhat specific (e.g., "Design teams")
- [ ] No - Too broad (e.g., "Any team" or "Everyone")

---

**2.2 Would your target audience recognize themselves in your messaging?**
- [ ] Yes - Uses language they use, addresses their specific problems
- [ ] Partial - Relatable but generic
- [ ] No - Doesn't speak to specific audience

---

**2.3 Is your product category clear?**
- [ ] Yes - Explicitly stated or obviously implied
- [ ] Partial - Can be inferred with effort
- [ ] No - Unclear what category you're in

---

**2.4 If you're creating a new category, do you have >$50M budget?**
- [ ] Yes - Well-funded for category creation
- [ ] N/A - Using established category
- [ ] No - Creating category without budget (⚠️ Warning!)

---

**2.5 Is your differentiation specific and provable?**
- [ ] Yes - Binary or very specific ("We do X, they don't")
- [ ] Partial - Somewhat specific but hard to prove
- [ ] No - Generic claims ("better UX," "innovative")

---

**2.6 Can you complete this statement: "Unlike [competitor/alternative], we [specific difference]"?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear, specific difference
- [ ] Partial - Vague difference
- [ ] No - Can't identify clear difference

---

## SECTION 3: Messaging - Features, Capabilities, Benefits

**3.1 Do you lead with capabilities (what users can DO) rather than generic benefits?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear action statements ("Design simultaneously," "Schedule with a link")
- [ ] Partial - Mix of capabilities and benefits
- [ ] No - Lead with vague benefits ("Save time," "Increase revenue")

---

**3.2 Are your capabilities specific and differentiated?**
- [ ] Yes - Capabilities competitors don't offer
- [ ] Partial - Some unique, some table stakes
- [ ] No - All capabilities are table stakes

---

**3.3 Do you explain HOW capabilities deliver benefits?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear capability → benefit connection
- [ ] Partial - Benefits stated but connection unclear
- [ ] No - Benefits without capabilities or vice versa

---

**3.4 Are features used to support capabilities (not lead)?**
- [ ] Yes - Features mentioned to explain how capabilities work
- [ ] Partial - Features and capabilities mixed together
- [ ] No - Lead with features or features not mentioned at all

---

**3.5 Are benefits specific and quantified where possible?**
- [ ] Yes - "Ship 2x faster," "Save 5 hours/week"
- [ ] Partial - "Ship faster," "Save time" (directional but not quantified)
- [ ] No - Generic or no benefits stated

---

**SECTION 3 SCORE**: _____ / 10 points

**If score is <7**: Review the Features-Capabilities-Benefits framework. Use `03-features-capabilities-benefits.md`

## SECTION 4: Homepage Structure & Flow

**4.1 Does your homepage follow a logical flow?**
- [ ] Yes - Hero → Problem → Solution → Value Props → Proof → CTA
- [ ] Partial - Most elements present but not in optimal order
- [ ] No - Random order or missing key sections

---

**4.2 Is there a problem section that articulates current pain?**
- [ ] Yes - Specific problems with current alternatives
- [ ] Partial - Generic problems mentioned
- [ ] No - No problem section

---

**4.3 Do you have 3-5 major value proposition sections?**
- [ ] Yes - 3-5 clear value props with depth
- [ ] Partial - Too few (<3) or too many (>5)
- [ ] No - No clear value prop structure

---

**4.4 Does each value prop follow Capability → Feature → Benefit?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear structure for each
- [ ] Partial - Some follow structure, some don't
- [ ] No - No consistent structure

---

**4.5 Are value props supported by visuals (screenshots, demos)?**
- [ ] Yes - Each value prop has relevant visual
- [ ] Partial - Some have visuals
- [ ] No - No visuals or generic stock photos

---

**4.6 Is social proof included (logos, testimonials, case studies)?**
- [ ] Yes - Multiple types of social proof
- [ ] Partial - One type (e.g., just logos)
- [ ] No - No social proof

---

**4.7 Are testimonials specific and quantified?**
- [ ] Yes - "We ship 2x faster" with name and company
- [ ] Partial - Generic praise ("Great product!")
- [ ] No - No testimonials

---

**4.8 Is there a clear final CTA?**
- [ ] Yes - Clear call to action with friction reducers
- [ ] Partial - CTA present but high friction
- [ ] No - No clear CTA or confusing

---

**If score is <12**: Review homepage structure guide. Use `04-homepage-structure-flow.md`

## SCORE INTERPRETATION

### 60-70 points: Excellent
Your homepage messaging is strong. Focus on optimization and testing.

**Next steps**:
- A/B test variations of hero section
- Deepen value prop content
- Add more social proof
- Review `12-testing-validation.md`

### 45-59 points: Good, Needs Refinement
Solid foundation but room for improvement in specific areas.

**Next steps**:
- Address sections scoring <75%
- Clarify differentiation
- Improve capability → benefit connections
- Review relevant core guides

### 30-44 points: Needs Significant Work
Major gaps in positioning or messaging.

**Next steps**:
- Start with positioning foundation (`01-positioning-foundation.md`)
- Fill out messaging canvas (`messaging-canvas-template.md`)
- Rewrite hero section (`05-hero-section-mastery.md`)
- Rebuild homepage structure (`04-homepage-structure-flow.md`)

### <30 points: Complete Overhaul Needed
Fundamental positioning and messaging problems.

**Next steps**:
1. Define Minimum Viable Positioning (`minimum-viable-positioning.md`)
2. Choose target audience (`10-target-audience-selection.md`)
3. Work through Positioning Foundation (`01-positioning-foundation.md`)
4. Build messaging from scratch (`messaging-canvas-template.md`)
5. Consider working with FletchPMM or similar expert
