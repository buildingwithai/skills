# Sales Onboarding and Ramp-Up

Complete guide to building world-class sales onboarding programs that reduce ramp time and accelerate new hire productivity.

## Overview

Sales onboarding is the structured process of preparing new sales hires to be successful, productive members of the team. It's the highest-ROI investment in sales enablement.

**Why Onboarding Matters**:
- **54% productivity increase** with formal onboarding programs
- **34% faster time to proficiency** with structured approach
- **20% faster ramp** with mentorship programs (HBR study)
- **58% higher 3-year retention** with good onboarding
- First 90 days determine long-term success or early departure

**Cost of Poor Onboarding**:
- Extended ramp time = delayed revenue (6 months vs 3.5 months = $50-120K per rep)
- Early turnover (20-30% of new hires leave in first year without good onboarding)
- Lost opportunity cost (reps spending 6+ months to reach productivity)

## Ramp Time Benchmarks

### Industry Standards (2024-2025)

**Sales Development Reps (SDRs)**:
- Average ramp time: **3.1 months** (Industry benchmark)
- Top quartile: **2 months**
- World-class: **1.5 months**

**Account Executives (AEs)**:
- Average ramp time: **4.9 months** (Industry benchmark)
- Top quartile: **3.5 months**
- World-class: **2.5 months**

**Sales Engineers (SEs)**:
- Average ramp time: **6-12 months** (more technical, product complexity)
- Can take up to 2 years to be fully operational in complex environments

**Target**: Reduce ramp time by **25-35%** with formal onboarding program

**Ramp Time Definition**: Time from start date to when rep consistently achieves 75-100% of quota

### Factors Affecting Ramp Time

**Accelerators** (shorter ramp):
- Formal onboarding program
- Structured curriculum with clear milestones
- Mentorship and shadowing
- Sales experience (prior industry or role experience)
- Simple product (easy to learn and demo)
- Short sales cycle (faster feedback loops)
- Strong enablement support

**Inhibitors** (longer ramp):
- No formal onboarding (ad hoc, figure it out yourself)
- Complex product (technical, multi-product, customizable)
- Long sales cycle (6-18 months = slower learning)
- First sales job (learning to sell + product + process)
- Weak manager support
- Poor tools or systems

## The 90-Day Onboarding Framework

### Week-by-Week Structure

**Weeks 1-2: Foundation**
- Company, culture, and values
- Product overview (what we sell, who we sell to, why they buy)
- Sales process and methodology overview
- Tools and systems setup (CRM, enablement platform, email, calendar)
- Meet the team (sales, marketing, product, CS)

**Weeks 3-4: Product Deep Dive**
- Detailed product training
- Use cases and customer stories
- Demo environment setup and practice
- Competitive landscape
- Pricing and packaging

**Weeks 5-6: Sales Skills**
- Discovery and qualification (methodology training)
- Demo and presentation skills
- Objection handling
- Negotiation basics
- Call and email best practices

**Weeks 7-8: Shadowing and Practice**
- Shadow top performers (10+ calls/meetings)
- Reverse shadowing (give demos with support)
- Role-play scenarios
- Feedback and coaching
- Refine talk track and approach

**Weeks 9-12: Ramping to Independence**
- First solo opportunities (with manager oversight)
- Weekly 1:1s with manager
- Peer learning and sharing
- Ongoing product and skill training
- Certification and graduation

**Goal by Day 90**:
- Fully proficient in product and demo
- Methodology adopted and applied
- Independently managing opportunities
- 50-75% of quota achievement (depending on sales cycle)
- Clear path to 100% quota achievement

### Daily Structure (First 2 Weeks)

**Typical Day**:
- **Morning (9am-12pm)**: Structured learning
  - Live training sessions (product, process, tools)
  - Self-paced modules (enablement platform)
  - Certifications and assessments
- **Lunch (12pm-1pm)**: Team connection
  - Lunch with team members, cross-functional partners
  - Informal Q&A and relationship building
- **Afternoon (1pm-4pm)**: Application and practice
  - Hands-on exercises (CRM, demo practice)
  - Shadowing calls and meetings
  - Homework and preparation for next day
- **End of Day (4pm-5pm)**: Reflection and planning
  - Daily check-in with manager or buddy
  - Questions and feedback
  - Prepare for tomorrow

**Balance**: 60% learning, 40% doing (first 2 weeks) → shift to 20% learning, 80% doing by week 8

## Curriculum Components

### 1. Company and Culture (Week 1)

**Topics**:
- Company history, mission, vision, values
- Organizational structure and key leaders
- Products and services overview
- Target markets and ideal customer profile
- Company culture and operating principles

**Delivery**:
- CEO welcome video or live session
- Self-paced company overview modules
- Meet & greets with leadership
- Culture handbook review

**Outcome**: New hire understands company context, feels connected to mission, knows who's who

---

### 2. Product Knowledge (Weeks 2-4)

**Topics**:
- Product features, functionality, and architecture
- Use cases and customer stories
- Value proposition by persona
- Integrations and ecosystem
- Pricing, packaging, and licensing
- Roadmap and upcoming releases

**Delivery**:
- Product training sessions (live and recorded)
- Demo environment hands-on practice
- Customer story reviews
- Product certification assessment

**Outcome**: New hire can confidently demo product, articulate value, answer basic questions

**Depth by Role**:
- **SDRs**: High-level value prop, key differentiators, qualification questions (1-2 weeks)
- **AEs**: Deep product knowledge, demo proficiency, use case mapping (3-4 weeks)
- **SEs**: Technical depth, architecture, integration, customization (6-8 weeks+)

---

### 3. Sales Process and Methodology (Weeks 3-4)

**Topics**:
- Sales stages and definitions
- Qualification framework (MEDDIC, SPICED, BANT - see `09-sales-methodologies.md`)
- Discovery question frameworks
- Opportunity management in CRM
- Forecasting and pipeline hygiene
- Deal review process

**Delivery**:
- Sales process training (live)
- Methodology certification
- CRM walkthrough and practice
- Manager-led deal review session

**Outcome**: New hire understands how to qualify, advance, and close deals using company process

---

### 4. Sales Skills Development (Weeks 4-6)

**Topics**:
- Discovery and needs analysis
- Effective questioning (SPIN, open vs closed)
- Active listening and note-taking
- Demo and presentation skills
- Storytelling and value articulation
- Objection handling
- Negotiation fundamentals
- Closing techniques

**Delivery**:
- Skills workshops (live)
- Role-play exercises
- Call recording reviews
- Peer feedback and coaching

**Outcome**: New hire has foundational sales skills and confidence to engage prospects

---

### 5. Tools and Systems (Week 1-2, ongoing)

**Topics**:
- **CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot)**: Logging activities, managing opportunities, forecasting
- **Enablement Platform (Highspot, Seismic)**: Finding content, tracking usage
- **Conversation Intelligence (Gong, Chorus)**: Recording calls, reviewing feedback
- **Email/Calendar**: Templates, scheduling, best practices
- **Prospecting Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo, etc.
- **Demo Environment**: Access, setup, troubleshooting

**Delivery**:
- Tool-specific training sessions
- Hands-on exercises and scavenger hunts
- Quick reference guides and cheat sheets
- IT/Ops support for setup

**Outcome**: New hire proficient in all required tools, can navigate and use independently

---

### 6. Market, Customers, and Competition (Weeks 2-4)

**Topics**:
- Target market segments and personas
- Ideal customer profile (ICP)
- Common pain points and buying triggers
- Customer success stories and case studies
- Competitive landscape
- Battle cards and competitive positioning (see `07-battle-cards-competitive-intelligence.md`)

**Delivery**:
- Market and persona training
- Customer story reviews
- Competitive training and certification
- Win/loss analysis reviews

**Outcome**: New hire understands who we sell to, why they buy, and how we win against competition

---

### 7. Content and Messaging (Weeks 3-4)

**Topics**:
- Value propositions by persona
- Key messaging pillars
- Sales playbooks (see `06-sales-playbooks.md`)
- Pitch decks and presentations
- One-pagers and leave-behinds
- Email templates and sequences
- Case studies and proof points

**Delivery**:
- Messaging workshop
- Playbook review and practice
- Content scavenger hunt (find key assets)
- Practice pitches and feedback

**Outcome**: New hire can articulate value clearly, knows where to find content, uses company messaging

## Learning Modalities

### 1. Live / Synchronous Training

**Format**: Instructor-led sessions (virtual or in-person)

**Best For**:
- Complex topics requiring discussion and Q&A
- Skills practice and role-plays
- Team building and connection
- Certifications and assessments

**Frequency**: Daily (first 2 weeks), 2-3x per week (weeks 3-8), weekly+ (ongoing)

**Examples**:
- Product deep dives with product team
- Sales methodology training
- Demo practice and feedback
- Skills workshops (discovery, objection handling)

---

### 2. Self-Paced / Asynchronous Learning

**Format**: On-demand modules, videos, reading

**Best For**:
- Product knowledge (can review multiple times)
- Company and culture orientation
- Tool training (watch, then practice)
- Reinforcement and reference

**Platform**: Enablement platform (Highspot, Seismic) or LMS

**Examples**:
- CEO welcome video
- Product feature overviews
- CRM training modules
- Competitive deep dives

**Best Practice**: Chunk content into 5-15 minute modules (microlearning)

---

### 3. Shadowing and Observation

**Format**: New hire observes experienced reps in action

**Best For**:
- Seeing sales process in practice
- Learning talk tracks and techniques
- Understanding customer interactions
- Building confidence

**Structure**:
- **Weeks 7-8**: Shadow 10-15 calls/meetings (discovery, demo, negotiation)
- **Note-taking**: What questions were asked? How were objections handled? What worked?
- **Debrief**: Discuss with rep after call (what was the strategy?)

**Top Performer Shadowing**: Prioritize shadowing best reps (learn winning behaviors)

---

### 4. Reverse Shadowing and Practice

**Format**: New hire leads with experienced rep observing and supporting

**Best For**:
- Building confidence before going solo
- Real-world practice with safety net
- Immediate feedback and coaching
- Gradual handoff to independence

**Structure**:
- **Week 8**: New hire gives demo with SE or AE supporting
- **Weeks 9-10**: New hire leads discovery with manager on call
- **Week 11-12**: New hire solo with manager reviewing after

**Feedback Loop**: Record calls, review together, identify improvement areas

---

### 5. Role-Play and Simulation

**Format**: Simulated sales scenarios with peers or managers

**Best For**:
- Practicing skills in safe environment
- Handling objections and tough questions
- Getting comfortable with talk tracks
- Building muscle memory

**Scenarios**:
- Cold call role-play
- Discovery call with challenging prospect
- Demo with technical objections
- Negotiation and pricing discussion
- Competitive displacement scenario

**Best Practice**: Record role-plays, review and provide specific feedback

---

### 6. Mentorship and Buddy Program

**Format**: Pairing new hire with experienced rep

**Impact**: **20% faster ramp** with mentorship (HBR study)

**Mentor Responsibilities**:
- Weekly check-ins (30 min)
- Answer questions and provide guidance
- Share experiences and lessons learned
- Advocate and support

**Buddy Responsibilities** (peer-level):
- Daily check-ins (first 2 weeks)
- Lunch and coffee connections
- Introduce to team and culture
- Be go-to person for "dumb questions"

**Selection**: Choose mentors who are top performers, patient teachers, and culture carriers

**Recognition**: Compensate or recognize mentors (part of performance review, bonus consideration)

## Measurement and Tracking

### Onboarding Completion Metrics

**Track**:
- % of curriculum completed: Target 90%+ by day 90
- Certification pass rates: Target 90%+ first-time pass
- Time spent in training: Track via LMS/enablement platform
- Attendance at live sessions: Target 95%+ attendance
- Engagement scores: Surveys after each module

**Red Flags**:
- <80% completion → Investigate barriers, adjust content
- <70% certification pass rate → Content too hard or poorly designed
- Low attendance → Schedule conflicts or lack of engagement

---

### Ramp Time Metrics

**Track**:
- **Time to first activity**: First logged call, email, meeting (should be week 1-2)
- **Time to first demo**: First product demo given (target: week 6-8)
- **Time to first opportunity**: First qualified opp created (target: week 8-12)
- **Time to first close**: First deal closed (varies by sales cycle, target: 3-6 months)
- **Time to productivity**: When rep hits 75-100% of quota consistently

**Benchmark**: Compare cohorts over time, identify improvements

**Example Dashboard**:
- Current cohort average ramp time: 3.8 months (vs 4.9 month baseline = 23% improvement)
- Onboarding completion rate: 92%
- Time to first demo: 7.2 weeks (vs 10 week baseline)
- 90-day retention: 95% (vs 85% baseline)

---

### Quality Metrics

**Assess**:
- Manager satisfaction with new hire readiness (survey after 90 days)
- New hire confidence scores (self-assessment at 30, 60, 90 days)
- Peer feedback (how did shadowing go?)
- Customer feedback (from early calls/demos - ask manager to spot-check)

**Certifications**:
- Product certification: Pass/fail, score
- Methodology certification: Pass/fail, practical assessment
- Demo certification: Manager observation, rubric-based scoring

**Target**: 90%+ of new hires pass certifications and receive "ready" rating from manager by day 90

## Onboarding Best Practices

### 1. Start Before Day 1 (Preboarding)

**Send Before Start Date**:
- Welcome email from CEO/Sales Leader
- Swag box (company merch, laptop, welcome kit)
- Pre-reading materials (company overview, product one-pagers)
- First week schedule
- IT/access instructions

**Benefits**:
- New hire feels welcomed and excited
- Hits ground running on day 1
- Reduces first-day anxiety

---

### 2. Cohort-Based Onboarding

**Group new hires** who start around same time (within 2-4 week window)

**Benefits**:
- Peer learning and support
- Builds camaraderie and friendly competition
- Efficient use of training resources
- Team cohesion from day 1

**Structure**: Hire in cohorts of 3-8 reps quarterly or as needed

---

### 3. Manager Involvement

**Manager is primary coach** (not enablement)

**Manager Responsibilities**:
- Welcome call before day 1
- Daily check-ins first 2 weeks
- Weekly 1:1s throughout onboarding
- Observe demos and provide feedback
- Review certifications and assessments
- Make go/no-go decision at 90 days

**Enablement Role**: Provide curriculum, content, and programs; train the trainers (managers)

---

### 4. Milestone-Based Progression

**Don't just track time** - track competency milestones

**Example Milestones**:
- ✅ Week 2: Product demo certified (can demo confidently)
- ✅ Week 4: Methodology certified (understands qualification framework)
- ✅ Week 6: First shadowed discovery call completed
- ✅ Week 8: First reverse shadow demo delivered
- ✅ Week 10: First qualified opportunity created
- ✅ Week 12: Manager approval to operate independently

**Gating**: New hire doesn't progress until milestone achieved (ensures quality)

---

### 5. Continuous Feedback and Adjustment

**Gather feedback**:
- New hire surveys (what's working? what's confusing?)
- Manager feedback (are new hires prepared?)
- Win/loss data (do new hires close at similar rates?)

**Iterate**:
- Update curriculum quarterly
- Refine based on data
- A/B test approaches (e.g., earlier shadowing vs later)

**Example Improvement**:
- Feedback: New hires struggling with objection handling
- Action: Add weekly objection role-play in weeks 5-8
- Result: Confidence scores improve 25%, certification pass rate increases

---

### 6. Celebrate Milestones and Graduation

**Recognize progress**:
- Week 4: Product certification celebration (certificate, Slack shoutout)
- Week 8: First demo delivered (manager recognition)
- Day 90: Graduation (cohort celebration, executive recognition)

**Graduation Event**:
- Present to leadership (demo or pitch)
- Receive certificate or recognition
- Celebrate with team (team lunch, happy hour)
- Transition from onboarding to ongoing development

## Common Onboarding Pitfalls

### 1. Drinking from the Firehose

**Problem**: Too much information, too fast → overwhelmed new hires

**Solution**:
- Prioritize must-know vs nice-to-know
- Spread content over 90 days, not 2 weeks
- Use microlearning (small chunks)
- Reinforce and repeat key concepts

---

### 2. Death by PowerPoint

**Problem**: Passive learning, boring slides, no interaction

**Solution**:
- Mix modalities (live, self-paced, shadowing, practice)
- Keep sessions interactive (Q&A, polls, discussions)
- Hands-on practice (not just watch and listen)
- Make it engaging and fun

---

### 3. No Clear Milestones or Progression

**Problem**: New hire doesn't know if they're on track or falling behind

**Solution**:
- Clear milestones with dates (see milestone-based progression above)
- Weekly check-ins with manager on progress
- Dashboard showing completion and readiness
- Transparent criteria for graduation

---

### 4. Sink or Swim After 90 Days

**Problem**: Great onboarding, then abandoned → reps struggle alone

**Solution**: Onboarding is first 90 days, but ongoing enablement continues
- Monthly product and competitive training
- Quarterly skills workshops
- Ongoing coaching and manager support
- Career development path beyond onboarding

---

### 5. One-Size-Fits-All

**Problem**: Same onboarding for SDRs, AEs, SEs → doesn't fit anyone well

**Solution**:
- Core curriculum (company, product, tools) for all
- Role-specific tracks (SDR focus on prospecting, AE on closing, SE on technical)
- Experience-based adjustments (first-time seller needs more sales skills, experienced seller needs more product)
