# Glossary: PMM Acronyms

Quick reference for common Product Marketing acronyms used throughout this skill.

## B2B/Sales Acronyms

**ICP** - Ideal Customer Profile
- The specific type of company that gets the most value from your product
- Example: "Mid-market B2B SaaS companies with 50-200 employees"

**TAM** - Total Addressable Market
- Total market demand for a product or service
- The revenue opportunity if you had 100% market share

**SAM** - Serviceable Addressable Market
- The portion of TAM you can realistically serve with your product/distribution

**SOM** - Serviceable Obtainable Market
- The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in the near term

**AE** - Account Executive
- Sales role responsible for closing deals

**SDR** - Sales Development Representative
- Sales role responsible for qualifying leads and booking meetings

**CS** - Customer Success
- Team responsible for customer retention and expansion

**ARR** - Annual Recurring Revenue
- Yearly value of recurring revenue (subscriptions)

**MRR** - Monthly Recurring Revenue
- Monthly value of recurring revenue

## Stakeholder Types (from references/core/03-multi-audience-messaging.md)

**P1** - Primary User (End User)
- Person who uses the product day-to-day
- Cares about: Does it work? Is it easy?

**P2** - Champion (Internal Advocate)
- Person who advocates for the solution internally
- Cares about: Can I sell this to my organization? Will it make me look good?

**P3** - Economic Buyer (Decision Maker)
- Person who approves the budget
- Cares about: What's the ROI? What's the risk?

## Sales Methodologies

**MEDDIC** - Sales qualification framework
- **M**etrics: What's the economic impact?
- **E**conomic Buyer: Who has the budget?
- **D**ecision Criteria: How will they decide?
- **D**ecision Process: What's their buying process?
- **I**dentify Pain: What problem are they solving?
- **C**hampion: Who's advocating for you internally?

**MEDDICC** - Extended MEDDIC (adds Competition)
- Same as MEDDIC + **C**ompetition: Who else are they evaluating?

**BANT** - Sales qualification framework
- **B**udget: Do they have funding?
- **A**uthority: Can they make the decision?
- **N**eed: Do they have the pain?
- **T**imeline: When will they decide?

## Research & Product Methodologies

**JTBD** - Jobs-to-be-Done
- Framework for understanding what customers "hire" products to do
- Focus on the job/outcome, not the product features

**MVP** - Minimum Viable Product
- Simplest version that delivers value and tests assumptions

**PMF** - Product-Market Fit
- When your product satisfies a strong market demand

## Messaging & Positioning

**USP** - Unique Selling Proposition
- What makes you different/better than alternatives

**Value Prop** - Value Proposition
- Statement of the value you deliver to customers

**UVP** - Unique Value Proposition
- Same as USP (terms used interchangeably)

**CTA** - Call-to-Action
- What you want the audience to do next (e.g., "Book a demo")

## Workshop & Process Terms

**HiPPO** - Highest Paid Person's Opinion
- When decisions are driven by seniority rather than data/customer input
- Example: "We need to manage the HiPPO in this positioning workshop"

**POV** - Point of View
- A specific perspective or stance on an issue

**SME** - Subject Matter Expert
- Person with deep expertise in a specific domain

## Marketing Channels

**SEM** - Search Engine Marketing
- Paid search advertising (Google Ads, etc.)

**SEO** - Search Engine Optimization
- Organic search visibility

**PPC** - Pay-Per-Click
- Advertising model where you pay per click

**CPC** - Cost-Per-Click
- How much you pay for each ad click

**CPL** - Cost-Per-Lead
- How much you pay for each lead generated

**CAC** - Customer Acquisition Cost
- Total cost to acquire a customer

**LTV** - Lifetime Value
- Total revenue expected from a customer over their lifetime

## Competitive Terms

**FUD** - Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt
- Competitive tactic of spreading negative information
- Example: "Competitor is using FUD about our security"

**TCO** - Total Cost of Ownership
- All costs associated with buying, implementing, and maintaining a solution
