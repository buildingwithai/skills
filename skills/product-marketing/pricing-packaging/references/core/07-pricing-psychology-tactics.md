# Pricing Psychology Tactics

Use behavioral economics to optimize pricing presentation and conversion.

## Overview

Pricing is not just math—it's psychology. How you present pricing affects customer perception of value, willingness to pay, and conversion rates.

**2024 Research:** Pricing psychology tactics can improve conversion 10-20% without changing the actual price.

**Warning:** Use ethics. These tactics should highlight genuine value, not manipulate.

## Core Psychological Principles

### 1. Anchoring

**Principle:** First price seen sets expectations for all subsequent prices.

**How it works:** Show high price first, everything else looks reasonable by comparison.

**Example: SaaS Pricing Page**
```
Enterprise: $2,999/month (shown first, top of page)
Business: $399/month (looks like great deal vs Enterprise)
Pro: $149/month (looks very affordable vs Business)
```

Without Enterprise tier: Pro at $149 might feel expensive.
With Enterprise tier: Pro at $149 feels like steal.

**2024 research:** Adding premium tier (+30% above target tier) increases conversions to target tier by 12-18%.

**Application:**
- Show highest-priced tier first (left-to-right or top)
- Add premium/enterprise tier even if few buy it (anchor)
- Show "List price $499" crossed out, "Now $299" (anchor to higher price)

---

### 2. Decoy Effect (Asymmetric Dominance)

**Principle:** Add third option that makes target option look like best value.

**Classic example:**
- Option A: Small popcorn $3
- Option B: Large popcorn $7
- **Option C (decoy): Medium popcorn $6.50**

Option C is "dominated" by B (pay almost same for less) → makes B look like best value.

**SaaS example:**
- Pro: $99/month (10 users, basic features)
- **Business: $149/month (unlimited users, advanced features)** ← Target
- Business Lite: $139/month (unlimited users, NO advanced features) ← Decoy

Business dominates Lite ($10 more but way more value) → everyone chooses Business.

**2024 research (Emerald Insight):** Decoy pricing increases target tier selection by 15-25%.

**Application:**
- Introduce slightly worse tier at almost same price as target
- Make target tier "obviously better" value
- Don't make decoy so bad it seems fake

---

### 3. Price Framing

**Principle:** Same price, different presentation, different perception.

**Examples:**

**Annual vs Monthly framing:**
- ❌ "$1,188/year" (feels expensive)
- ✅ "$99/month billed annually" (feels affordable)

**Daily breakdown:**
- ✅ "Less than $3/day" for $99/month product (sounds tiny)

**Per-user framing:**
- "$500/month for 10 users" 
- vs "Just $50 per user/month" (sounds more affordable)

**Savings framing:**
- "Save $240/year with annual billing" (emphasizes savings)
- vs "16% discount" (less compelling)

**Application:**
- Frame annual as monthly ("$99/month" not "$1,188/year")
- Break down to daily/per-user when it makes product seem affordable
- Emphasize savings in dollars, not percentages

---

### 4. Charm Pricing ($99 vs $100)

**Principle:** Prices ending in 9 perceived as significantly cheaper.

**Research:** $99 feels ~20% cheaper than $100 (even though it's only 1% less).

**When charm pricing works:**
- **B2C, SMB:** $19, $49, $99, $199 (very effective)
- **Mid-market:** $499, $999, $2,499 (moderately effective)

**When to avoid:**
- **Enterprise:** $99,999 feels cheap/gimmicky (use $100,000)
- **Premium positioning:** $1,000 feels more premium than $999
- **High-trust industries:** Legal, finance (round numbers feel more trustworthy)

**Application:**
- SMB tiers: Use $49, $99, $299
- Enterprise: Use round numbers ($500, $1,000, $2,500)
- Premium products: Round numbers signal quality

---

### 5. Center Stage Effect

**Principle:** Middle option gets most attention and selection.

**Reason:** When unsure, people choose middle (Goldilocks effect).

**Data:** Middle tier converts 60-70% of customers (vs 15-20% for lowest, 15-20% for highest).

**Application:**
- Design target tier as middle tier
- Visually highlight middle tier (color, badge, "Most Popular")
- Price accordingly (middle = where you want most customers)

**Example: Notion**
```
Plus: $10/user/month (middle tier, target)
  ↑ Highlighted, "Most Popular" badge

Free: Below (anchor low)
Business: Above (anchor high)
```

---

### 6. Bundle Pricing (Value Perception)

**Principle:** Bundled price feels like better value than à la carte.

**Example:**
- Individual purchase: Feature A ($50) + Feature B ($50) + Feature C ($50) = $150
- Bundle: "All features $99/month" (feels like $51 saved)

**Application:**
- Don't price features individually
- Bundle features in tiers
- Highlight "value" of bundle ("$300 value, now $199")

**Warning:** Avoid fake anchoring ("$10,000 value" when no one pays that).

## Pricing Page Psychology

### Visual Hierarchy

**Best practices:**

**1. Highlight target tier:**
- Contrasting color (everyone else gray, target bright blue)
- "Most Popular" or "Best Value" badge
- Subtle elevation or border

**2. Left-to-right flow (Western markets):**
- Highest price left → lowest price right
- Or lowest left → highest right (both work, test)

**3. Comparison table:**
- 5-8 key features only (not 20)
- Check marks for included features
- Visual hierarchy (important features at top)

---

### Copywriting Tactics

**Emphasize value, not price:**

❌ "Only $299/month"
✅ "Unlimited users, advanced features, priority support—$299/month"

**Use social proof:**
- "Join 10,000+ companies" (credibility)
- "Trusted by [logos]" (trust)
- "4.8/5 stars from 2,000 reviews" (validation)

**Create urgency (ethically):**
- "Limited time: 20% off annual plans" (real urgency)
- ❌ "Only 3 spots left!" (fake scarcity = unethical)

**Address objections:**
- "No credit card required for free trial" (removes friction)
- "Cancel anytime" (reduces risk)
- "14-day money-back guarantee" (safety net)

---

### Price Comparison (Competitive)

**When to show competitor prices:**
✅ You're significantly cheaper (show savings)
✅ You're premium but justify value (show what you include vs them)

**When NOT to show:**
❌ You're more expensive and can't justify
❌ Competitors not well-known (confusing, not helpful)

**How to compare:**

**Good example:**
```
Us: $99/month (includes A, B, C, D, E)
Competitor X: $79/month (includes A, B only)
Competitor Y: $120/month (includes A, B, C only)

Value: $99 for more features = best value
```

**Bad example:**
"Competitor is $50 cheaper but we're better" (not compelling)

## Discount Psychology

### When Discounts Work

**Annual billing discounts:**
- Standard: 16-20% off (2 months free)
- Messaging: "Save $240/year" (dollar amount > percentage)

**Volume discounts:**
- 10+ users: 10% off
- 50+ users: 20% off
- 100+ users: 30% off

**Launch discounts:**
- "Early adopter pricing: 20% off first year"
- Creates urgency, rewards early customers

---

### When Discounts Backfire

**Frequent discounting:**
- Trains customers to wait for sales
- Devalues product ("if it's always on sale, why pay full price?")

**Too steep discounts:**
- 50%+ discount = quality concerns ("why so cheap?")
- Attracts price-sensitive customers (higher churn)

**Better alternatives:**
- Add value instead of discount (bonus features, extended trial)
- Bundle (add second product free)
- Credits (give $500 usage credits)

See `advanced/discount-strategies-annual-pricing.md` for detailed strategies.

## Transparency vs Complexity

### Transparent Pricing (Trust)

**When to show pricing publicly:**
- ✅ SMB/mid-market ($50-$5,000/month)
- ✅ Self-serve products
- ✅ Standard packages

**Benefits:**
- 73% of SMB buyers won't engage without public pricing (2024 Paddle data)
- Builds trust
- Filters unqualified leads (self-qualification)

---

### "Contact Sales" (Enterprise)

**When to hide pricing:**
- ✅ True enterprise (>$100K deals)
- ✅ Highly variable pricing (depends on usage, customization)
- ✅ Complex procurement (RFP, legal review)

**Best practice:** Show pricing for lower tiers, "Contact sales" for Enterprise only.

**Example:**
```
Pro: $99/month (shown)
Business: $499/month (shown)
Enterprise: Contact Sales (custom pricing)
```

## Geographic & Cultural Considerations

### Currency Localization

**Show local currency:**
- US: $99/month
- UK: £79/month
- EU: €89/month

**Impact:** 15-20% higher conversion vs showing USD globally.

---

### Cultural Differences

**US/Canada:**
- Charm pricing works ($.99)
- Discounts effective

**Nordic countries:**
- Premium positioning resonates
- Less price-sensitive
- Can charge 20-30% more

**Germany:**
- Transparency valued
- Detailed feature comparison
- Price-sensitive despite high WTP

**Asia (India, SE Asia):**
- Price very sensitive
- Discounts expected
- Purchasing power parity pricing necessary

See `advanced/international-localized-pricing.md`.

## Ethical Considerations

### Do's ✅

- ✅ Highlight genuine value
- ✅ Use anchoring with real alternatives
- ✅ Create urgency with real deadlines
- ✅ Social proof from real customers
- ✅ Discounts that reflect real savings

### Don'ts ❌

- ❌ Fake scarcity ("Only 3 left!" when unlimited)
- ❌ Fake urgency ("Sale ends tonight!" every day)
- ❌ Inflated "original prices" never actually charged
- ❌ Dark patterns (hidden fees, hard to cancel)
- ❌ Bait-and-switch (advertise low price, force upgrades)

**Guideline:** Ask "Would I feel manipulated?" If yes, don't do it.

## A/B Testing Pricing Psychology

**What to test:**
- Tier order (high-to-low vs low-to-high)
- Highlighted tier (which one gets "Most Popular" badge)
- Framing (monthly vs annual, per-user vs total)
- Comparison table (features shown, order)
- CTA copy ("Get Started" vs "Try Free" vs "Start Trial")

**Sample size:** 250-500 visitors per variation, 30-60 days

See `core/09-pricing-experimentation-testing.md`.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Anchoring works** - Show premium tier to make target tier look affordable
2. **Decoy effect increases conversions 15-25%** - Add dominated option near target
3. **Middle tier converts 60-70%** - Design target as middle tier
4. **Charm pricing for SMB** ($99) **round numbers for enterprise** ($1,000)
5. **Frame annual as monthly** - "$99/month" not "$1,188/year"
6. **Transparency for SMB** - 73% won't engage without public pricing
7. **Use ethics** - Highlight value, don't manipulate
