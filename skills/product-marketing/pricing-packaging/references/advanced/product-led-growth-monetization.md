# Product-Led Growth Monetization

Optimize pricing for PLG (product-led growth) strategies.

## Overview

**Product-Led Growth (PLG):** Product itself drives acquisition, activation, and expansion (not sales/marketing).

**Characteristics:**
- Self-serve signup (no sales call)
- Free trial or freemium
- In-product upgrade prompts
- Viral/network effects
- Bottom-up adoption (individual â†’ team â†’ company)

**2024 data:**
- 58% of SaaS companies use PLG motion (up from 41% in 2021)
- PLG companies grow 30% faster than sales-led (OpenView 2024)
- Top PLG companies: Slack, Zoom, Notion, Figma, Calendly, Dropbox

## PLG Pricing Requirements

### Must-Haves for PLG Pricing

**1. Free entry point**
- Free tier (freemium) or free trial
- No gated demo, no sales call to start

**2. Self-serve purchase**
- Credit card checkout (instant access)
- No "Contact Sales" for core tiers

**3. Transparent pricing**
- Pricing page shows all prices
- No hidden costs

**4. Intuitive upgrade path**
- Clear differentiation (free vs paid)
- In-product prompts when limits hit

**5. Value-aligned pricing**
- Pay for value received (not arbitrary)
- Scales with customer growth

## PLG Conversion Triggers

### Trigger 1: Usage Limit Hit

**Example:**
- Free tier: 100 API calls/month
- User hits 100 calls
- Prompt: "You've reached your limit. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited calls."

**Timing:** Moment of friction (can't continue without upgrade)

**Conversion:** 8-12% convert when hitting hard limit

---

### Trigger 2: Gated Feature Click

**Example:**
- Free tier: Basic dashboards only
- User clicks "Advanced Analytics"
- Prompt: "Advanced Analytics available on Pro plan. [Upgrade]"

**Timing:** Moment of intent (wants feature now)

**Conversion:** 10-15% convert when clicking gated feature (high intent)

---

### Trigger 3: Team Invite

**Example:**
- Free tier: Solo use
- User invites 2nd person
- Prompt: "Collaboration requires Pro plan ($10/user/month). [Upgrade]"

**Timing:** Expansion moment (team > individual)

**Conversion:** 15-20% convert when inviting team

---

### Trigger 4: Time-Based Milestone

**Example:**
- Day 7: "You've created 10 projects! Power users love Pro for unlimited projects."
- Day 30: "You're a heavy user. Upgrade to remove limits and unlock [features]."

**Timing:** After "aha moment" (experienced value)

**Conversion:** 5-10% convert from time-based prompts

---

### Trigger 5: Behavioral Prompt

**Example:**
- User exports data 3x in one week
- Prompt: "Looks like you're exporting a lot. Pro plan includes automated exports. [Learn more]"

**Timing:** After repeated action (behavioral signal)

**Conversion:** 8-12% convert from behavioral prompts

## In-Product Monetization UX

### Upgrade Prompts

**Best practices:**

**1. Context-aware**
- Show prompt when relevant (not random)
- Example: Prompt for "Advanced Analytics" when user views dashboard (not when writing doc)

**2. Value-focused**
- Not: "Upgrade to Pro"
- Better: "Unlock advanced analytics to see trends. [Upgrade to Pro]"

**3. Non-intrusive**
- Don't block workflow (modal that forces click)
- Subtle banner or tooltip (can dismiss)

**4. Frequency-capped**
- Max 1-2 prompts per session
- Don't spam (trains users to ignore)

---

### Pricing Page Access

**In-product links:**
- Settings â†’ "Upgrade" button (always visible)
- Usage dashboard â†’ "Upgrade to increase limits"
- Help menu â†’ "View Plans & Pricing"

**Goal:** Easy to find when customer ready (don't hide)

---

### Trial-to-Paid Transition

**14-day trial countdown:**
- Day 10: "Trial ends in 4 days. Upgrade now to keep access."
- Day 14: "Trial ended. [Upgrade] to continue using [Product]."

**Micro-copy:**
- "No credit card required" (trial start)
- "14-day money-back guarantee" (purchase page)

## PLG Metrics

### 1. Free-to-Paid Conversion

**Formula:** (# of paying customers / # of free signups) Ã— 100

**Benchmark:**
- Freemium: 2-5% (average), 6-8% (good)
- Free trial: 15-25% (average), 30-40% (good)

**Track by:**
- Cohort (Jan signups â†’ % converted by Month 3, 6, 12)
- Channel (organic, paid, referral)
- Segment (SMB, enterprise)

---

### 2. Time to Paid

**Formula:** Median days from signup to first payment

**Benchmark:**
- Freemium: 30-90 days (slow burn)
- Free trial: 7-14 days (fast)

**Optimize:** Reduce time to paid (faster = better cash flow, less churn before pay)

---

### 3. Product Qualified Leads (PQLs)

**Definition:** Free users who match "likely to convert" criteria

**Criteria (example):**
- Activated (completed onboarding)
- Usage (10+ sessions, created 5+ projects)
- Team invite (invited 2+ teammates)
- Gated feature click (tried advanced feature)

**PQL conversion:** 20-40% of PQLs convert vs 2-5% of all free users

**Use:** Sales/CS outreach to PQLs (high intent)

---

### 4. Expansion Revenue (from PLG)

**Formula:** (Expansion MRR / Total MRR) Ã— 100

**Benchmark:** 30-40% of revenue from expansion (good PLG motion)

**Sources:**
- Seat expansion (add more users)
- Tier upgrades (Pro â†’ Business)
- Usage growth (usage-based pricing)

---

### 5. Viral Coefficient (k-factor)

**Formula:** (# of invites sent per user) Ã— (% of invites that sign up)

**Example:**
- Average user invites 3 people
- 20% of invites sign up
- k = 3 Ã— 0.20 = 0.6

**Interpretation:**
- k > 1: Viral growth (every user brings >1 new user)
- k < 1: Not viral (need paid acquisition)

**PLG goal:** Maximize k (product design for sharing/invites)

## PLG + Sales-Assist (Hybrid)

### When to Add Sales

**Triggers:**
- PQL reaches high usage (20+ users, $5K+ ACV potential)
- Enterprise domain (e.g., @ibm.com, @walmart.com)
- Manual outreach request (user clicks "Contact Sales")
- Behavior signals (trials SSO, asks about security)

**Sales role:**
- Not gatekeeper (product does acquisition)
- Expansion focus (upsell Pro â†’ Business â†’ Enterprise)
- White-glove (onboarding, training, custom contracts)

---

### PLG-Sales Handoff

**Step 1: Product alerts sales**
- PQL score reaches threshold (80/100)
- Alert: "Acme Corp (50 users, Pro tier) is a PQL. Reach out."

**Step 2: Sales researches**
- Reviews product usage (what features used, how often)
- Identifies expansion opportunities (need SSO? More users?)

**Step 3: Sales reaches out**
- Email: "Hi [name], I noticed your team is loving [Product]. I'd love to show you [Business tier features] that could help with [specific need]."
- Goal: Book call, not close immediately

**Step 4: Discovery & demo**
- Understand pain points, budget, decision process
- Demo Business/Enterprise tier
- Propose custom plan

**Step 5: Close**
- Negotiated pricing (multi-year, custom terms)
- Convert to sales-managed account
