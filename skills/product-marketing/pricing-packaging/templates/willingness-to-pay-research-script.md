# Willingness to Pay Research Script

Interview guide for pricing research with customers and prospects.

## Interview Setup

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Format:** 1-on-1 interview (phone, Zoom, or in-person)
**Incentive:** $50-100 gift card or product credit
**Sample size goal:** 30-50 interviews (minimum 20)

**Participant criteria:**
- [ ] Current customers (50%)
- [ ] Qualified prospects (50%)
- [ ] Mix of segments (SMB, mid-market, enterprise)
- [ ] Active users (used product in last 30 days)

---

## Interview Script

### Introduction (2 minutes)

```
Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to chat today!

I'm [Your Name] from [Company]. We're doing research to better 
understand how customers like you think about pricing for products 
like ours.

This conversation will take about 30 minutes. There are no right or 
wrong answers - I'm genuinely interested in your honest feedback.

Everything you share will be confidential. We'll use this input to 
improve our pricing, but won't share your individual responses.

Sound good? Great, let's get started.
```

---

### Part 1: Context & Usage (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their situation and product usage

**Questions:**

**1. Tell me about your role and what you're responsible for.**
[Listen for: budget authority, decision-making power, pain points]

**2. How are you currently using [Product]? What's your primary use case?**
[Listen for: frequency, depth, value derived]

**3. What problem does [Product] solve for you?**
[Listen for: quantifiable value, time saved, revenue impact]

**4. If [Product] didn't exist, what would you use instead?**
[Listen for: alternatives, competitive landscape, switching costs]

---

### Part 2: Van Westendorp PSM (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Establish price sensitivity range

**Setup:**
```
Now I'm going to ask you four questions about pricing. 

Imagine you're evaluating [Product] for the first time today. 
Think about the value it provides vs alternatives.

There are no wrong answers - just give me your gut reaction to each question.
```

**Question 1: At what price would you consider [Product] to be so expensive that you would not consider buying it? (Too expensive)**

$___________

[Follow-up: "What makes that price too expensive?"]

---

**Question 2: At what price would you consider [Product] to be priced so low that you would feel the quality couldn't be very good? (Too cheap)**

$___________

[Follow-up: "What concerns would you have at that price?"]

---

**Question 3: At what price would you consider [Product] to be starting to get expensive, but you'd still consider it? (Getting expensive)**

$___________

[Follow-up: "What makes you hesitate at that price?"]

---

**Question 4: At what price would you consider [Product] to be a bargainâ€”a great buy for the money? (Great value)**

$___________

[Follow-up: "What makes that price a bargain?"]

---

**Summary:**
```
Just to confirm:
- Too expensive: $____
- Getting expensive: $____
- Great value: $____
- Too cheap: $____

Does that sound right?
```

### Part 4: Competitive Context (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand competitive pricing perception

**Question 8: Have you evaluated competitors to [Product]? If so, which ones?**

Competitors considered:
- ___________
- ___________

---

**Question 9: How does [Product] compare to [Competitor] in terms of value for price?**

- [ ] Much better value
- [ ] Somewhat better value
- [ ] About the same
- [ ] Somewhat worse value
- [ ] Much worse value

[Probe: "What makes you say that?"]

---

**Question 10: What would [Competitor] need to cost for you to switch from [Product]?**

$___________ (or ___% less than [Product])

[Probe: "What would make you switch besides price?"]

---

### Part 5: Willingness to Pay (Current vs New Features) (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand price elasticity and expansion WTP

**Question 11: At your current usage level, what's the maximum you'd be willing to pay for [Product]?**

$___________ per month / per year / per user

[Probe: "What would make you willing to pay more?"]

---

**Question 12: If we added [Premium Feature X], how much more would you be willing to pay?**

Additional WTP: $___________

[Repeat for 2-3 key features you're considering for premium tiers]

Premium Feature Y: +$___________
Premium Feature Z: +$___________

---

### Part 6: Pricing Model Preference (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pricing model (per-user, usage-based, etc.)

**Question 13: How would you prefer to pay for [Product]?**

Present 2-3 options:

**Option A: Per-user pricing**
- $20/user/month
- Unlimited usage

**Option B: Usage-based pricing**
- $0.001 per API call
- Pay only for what you use

**Option C: Hybrid pricing**
- $100/month base (includes 100K API calls)
- $0.001 per additional API call

[Ask: "Which option do you prefer and why?"]
